## What this is

A single bridge brief that holds the five working artefacts of the NHS DTx commercial work in one place. It exists because the artefacts are being built in parallel and the work was starting to drift. This brief is the rule for how they fit together and the cardinal positions they all share.

This is not itself an artefact for external audiences. It is an internal coordination document for any chat, session or person working on any of the five spine artefacts.

## The cardinal positions

These are the positions every artefact must align against. They are sourced from the Position Papers dated 28 May 2026 and from settled language in the Commercial Playbook.

**Commercial framework: operating in interim shape.** No formal commercial framework is being stood up in the current window. The matchmaker-with-assurance model is the live operating shape. The originally targeted April 2027 standup window has been retired as unachievable in the current planning horizon. The framework conversation reopens at named triggers: portfolio approaches the scale where the take-rate materially earns its keep (working hypothesis around ten live tests on the approved list, specific threshold not yet locked), aggregate transacted volume crosses the scale where a percentage take becomes material (specific threshold not yet locked), suppliers actively ask for the structure, NHSE Legal's position on the interim vehicle changes, the FY27 planning round sets the next target standup window. The PA23 dynamic market is the leading candidate vehicle if the conversation reopens. PA23 is the procurement regime; the dynamic market is the specific vehicle pictured within it. It is not a committed destination. The earlier "paused" framing has been retired as stronger than the source-call language actually supports.

**NHSE role: orchestrator, not party.** NHSE convenes the system, assures supplier quality through the published standard, and introduces approved suppliers to commissioners. NHSE is not a party to the resulting contract. NHSE takes no percentage of the bilateral transaction between supplier and customer.

**MoU scope: alpha and beta only.** MoU is an instrument used for HIV alpha and beta proof points. It is not the BAU contracting vehicle. The BAU instrument is "contract / call-off" between supplier and commissioner.

**Engagement: integrated pre-sales cell.** Hearts and Minds work runs through one pre-sales cell under sponsor leadership, not a separate sales team. The cell carries relationship-building, road-show, patient stories and senior sponsor conversations. Commercial machinery engages only after the cell has warmed up a partner. This cell is the Hearts and Minds lane on the Map and the engagement-side plays in the Playbook. Position paper status reads "Assumed position: Commercial owned" because sponsor endorsement has not formally landed; the brief treats it as cardinal because Commercial is committed to it and presenting it forward.

**Assurance is primary, commercial is secondary.** The work that earns NHSE its place is the assurance layer: independent assurance (third-party discipline distinct from internal Clinical Safety, role defined in the DTx OM Governance lane), the NICE EVA conditional engagement and evaluation interface (already used to select HealthStore COPD Alpha suppliers), assurance portability ("assure once, deploy many" across ICBs and configurations), and the supplier IG envelope. The commercial layer is built on top of this. Investment sequence reflects this.

**Compelled removal is the enforcement mechanism.** Removal from the approved list is the only enforcement leverage NHSE holds in the interim model. There is no framework call-off and therefore no termination clause to invoke. The removal protocol must be defensible the first time it bites: clear failure thresholds, proportional escalation, evidence-led decision-making, named authority signs the removal, supplier right of appeal, commissioner notification, full audit trail.

**Existing routes for current proof points.** HIV Beta runs on a three-vehicle structure (NHSE-to-LA MoUs with Salford and East Sussex; NHSE-to-supplier via the ACE Framework call-off sourced through SBS Lot 1, SBS10255, per the signed contracts (Preventx at £100,000 ex VAT and SH:24 at £99,490 ex VAT, both T&M-capped at £100k per supplier with a £200k total Alpha budget envelope); LA-to-supplier for test delivery via existing sexual-health contracts (Salford with SH:24, East Sussex with Preventx)). HealthStore COPD Alpha runs on ACE with NHS Standard Contract carrying SaaS and data schedules; ICB-primary commissioning with clinician-prescribed initiation and episode-payment commercial structure. These were stood up to support proving the model, not to carry scale. They are not the commercial model. They transition via Play 7 (Beta-to-BAU transition) as each proof-point matures.

## The rule

**Each artefact references the others. None re-states the others' positions.**

- The Map's decision gates anchor in Position Papers as the formal record. The Map narrates time; the Papers carry the load-bearing position.
- The Map's Hearts and Minds character moments link to Playbook plays for the operational runbook. The Map shows when Sarah converts; the Playbook (Play 10) shows how.
- Playbook plays reference DTx OM components for the architectural detail (foundation chips, lifecycle stages, capability descriptions). Playbook describes operations against the architecture; OM describes the architecture itself.
- Position Papers cite Playbook plays where they came from and DTx OM components where they apply via a "Related architecture" section already in place on all nine papers.
- Sales packs draw from Hearts and Minds character pages in the OM. They do not invent new narrative. They re-tell the character stories for the room they are being told to.

If any artefact contradicts another, the Position Papers are authoritative on positions, the Playbook is authoritative on operational mechanics, the DTx OM is authoritative on architecture, and the Map is authoritative on time and sequencing. The sales packs always defer to the others.

## Working norms

1. **Read this brief before starting work on any spine artefact.**
2. **If a position changes, the Position Paper changes first.** Then the Map, Playbook and sales packs follow. Never the other way round.
3. **If a chat is unsure which artefact owns a piece of content, ask Scott.** Do not duplicate content into two artefacts to be safe.
4. **Backups before structural edits.** All five artefacts have backup discipline; honour it.
5. **No named individuals in artefacts.** Functional descriptors only. This brief is internal so it names Scott as owner; nothing else uses real names.
6. **Primary sources are authoritative on detail, and only signed documents count as primary source.** Contract values, party names, statutory dates and any other specific facts in this brief, in a Position Paper, in the Playbook or in any spine artefact must be verified against the signed document by filename: the signed call-off contract, the signed minute, the signed financial approval. Briefing notes, Knowledge Base entries, prior KB versions, slide-deck references and working summaries are NOT primary sources. They are working artefacts that can carry forward errors. If a number lacks a signed-document cite, it goes in a working note as "working estimate, source TBC", not into a spine artefact as fact. The 28 May 2026 £648k correction (see Knowledge Base `_update_log.md`) is the worked example of what happens when this discipline isn't applied: a wrong number repeated across five derived artefacts becomes treated as fact through repetition alone.
