Existing routes for current proof points

The current proof-point routes are interim arrangements stood up to support proving. They are not the commercial model. They transition to the matchmaker-with-assurance shape as the model matures.

StatusActive
OwnerHomeTest Commercial lead
Date taken28 May 2026
Review cadenceAs each proof-point exits Beta

Position

The current proof-point routes were stood up to support proving the model at small scale, not to carry portfolio scale. HIV Beta runs on a three-vehicle structure using the ACE Framework call-off (via SBS Lot 1, SBS10255), NHSE-to-LA MoUs, and LA-held sexual-health contracts with the test-delivery suppliers. HealthStore COPD Alpha runs on ACE as the procurement route with an NHS Standard Contract carrying SaaS and data schedules. These routes are interim. They are not the commercial model. They transition to the matchmaker-with-assurance shape via Play 7 (Beta-to-BAU transition) as each proof-point matures.

The current routes: primary-source detail

HIV Beta, three vehicles. NHSE-to-LA MoUs with Salford and East Sussex (no payment between NHSE and LAs). NHSE-to-supplier via the ACE Framework call-off sourced via SBS Lot 1 (SBS10255). Per the signed call-off contracts: Preventx £100,000 ex VAT (`ACE_PREVENTX_v2`); SH:24 £99,490 ex VAT inclusive of the 4% management fee SH:24 absorbs (`ACE_SH24_r2`). Both T&M-capped at £100k per supplier with a £200k total Alpha budget envelope available across the two. NHSE is counterparty for supplier integration and platform development only, not for the tests themselves. LA-to-supplier for test delivery via the LAs' existing sexual-health contracts (Salford with SH:24, East Sussex with Preventx). NHSE is not the commercial counterparty for the tests themselves. Public Beta commercial route is not yet defined and is an open item.

HealthStore COPD Alpha, ACE plus NHS Standard Contract. ACE Framework used as the Alpha procurement route, three bids received. Contract pattern is the NHS Standard Contract with SaaS and data schedules. Commissioning model is ICB-primary with clinician-prescribed initiation and an episode-payment commercial structure. Steady-state route post-Alpha is open: extend ACE, build a HealthStore-specific instrument, or piggy-back on another framework.

Rationale

Both proof-points were stood up under time pressure to demonstrate the operating model with a real cohort. ACE (Approved Care Ecosystem, an NHS Innovation Procurement Taskforce framework) was the available existing route that admits innovation-stage suppliers at speed. SBS Lot 1 was the call-off vehicle sitting underneath ACE for HomeTest HIV. None of these instruments were designed to carry portfolio scale or to define the BAU commercial architecture.

Holding the proof-point routes up as the commercial model would be a category error. They were chosen because they were available and fast, not because they reflect the matchmaker-with-assurance position. Treating them as the commercial model would lock the programme into the wrong vehicle and the wrong contracting shape.

The transition path is via Play 7 (Beta-to-BAU transition). As each proof-point matures, the existing arrangements novate or wind down onto the standard call-off plus approved-list shape. Until that transition happens, the existing routes carry on operating without prejudice to the position.

Reopen triggers

  • A proof-point route gets extended materially beyond Beta scale. At that point it stops being an interim and starts shaping the model by default.
  • A new proof-point is stood up using a different interim route not listed here. The position needs to absorb the new route or push back on its use.
  • Play 7 transition mechanics expose a gap that the existing-route position needs to address.
  • NHSE Legal concludes that one of the existing routes (ACE, SBS Lot 1) must be carried into BAU rather than transitioned off.

Dependencies

  • Play 7 (Beta-to-BAU transition) operational. The transition mechanism that moves proof-points off the interim routes and onto the standard call-off plus approved-list shape.
  • Standard call-off template authored. Receiving instrument for the transition. Without it the existing routes have nowhere to transition to.
  • Approved supplier list register stood up. Receiving qualification mechanism for suppliers currently admitted via ACE.
  • Public Beta commercial route defined (HIV). Open item; needed before HIV moves from Private Beta into Public Beta scale.
  • Public Beta Direction request (HIV). Per PTT (20 May 2026), no Direction is required for Private Beta because the data movement is system delivery (not NHSE-side analysis). Public Beta likely needs a Direction when NHSE begins to analyse data for its own purposes (cohort MI etc.). Lead time ~6 months. Direction request should land before January 2027 or after March 2027 to avoid the DHSC/NHSE legal-merger transition window.

What this position protects against

Two failure modes. First: the proof-point routes get treated as the commercial model by default because they are what exists, locking the programme into ACE-and-SBS-Lot-1 shape forever. Second: the proof-point routes get torn up prematurely before Play 7 transition mechanics are ready, leaving live cohorts without a contracting vehicle. The position holds the line in the middle: current routes carry on operating, transition happens through Play 7, and the matchmaker-with-assurance shape is the destination.
