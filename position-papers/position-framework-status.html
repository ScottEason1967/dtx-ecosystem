Position paper · HomeTest Commercial

Framework status: operating in interim shape

No formal commercial framework in the FY26–FY27 window. The commercial machinery is operating in the interim shape. Named reopen triggers govern when the framework conversation reopens.

StatusActive
OwnerHomeTest Commercial lead
Date taken28 May 2026
Review cadenceQuarterly portfolio review

Position

HomeTest is operating in the interim commercial shape. A formal commercial framework is not being stood up in this window. The commercial machinery operates through the Assurance wrapper (approved supplier list, published assurance standard, standard NHS call-off contract) with bilateral contracting between supplier and customer. NHSE convenes the system, assures supplier quality, and introduces approved suppliers to commissioners. NHSE is not a party to the resulting contract and takes no percentage of the bilateral transaction. The framework conversation reopens at named triggers. The originally targeted April 2027 standup window has been retired as unachievable in the current planning horizon.

Rationale

At interim portfolio scale of one to three live tests, take-rate revenues from a formal framework do not justify either the standup cost or the political surface of operating as a commercial counterparty. A multi-quarter standup window would also delay PSA scaling while market pressure from the men's health strategy builds. The cost of waiting is not absorbed by the value of the framework.

The matchmaker-with-assurance model preserves NHSE's authority over supplier quality (through the approved list and the published standard) without absorbing the commercial liability of being a contracting party. Bilateral contracting between supplier and commissioner uses existing NHS Standard Contract machinery. No new instruments to design, no novel legal architecture to defend.

Reopen triggers

The framework conversation reopens when any one of the following lands:

  • The portfolio approaches the scale where the take-rate would materially earn its keep. Working hypothesis around ten live tests on the approved list; the specific threshold is not yet locked.
  • Aggregate transacted volume across the portfolio crosses the scale where a percentage take becomes material. Specific volume threshold not yet locked.
  • Suppliers actively ask for the structure. The absence of a framework starts to constrain supplier behaviour or market entry.
  • NHSE Legal changes its position on the interim legal vehicle (see Position · Interim legal vehicle).
  • The FY27 planning round is reached and the next target standup window is set. Whichever position is taken at that point supersedes this paper.
  • A regulatory ruling, NAO finding or material patient-harm event challenges the matchmaker-without-framework architecture.

If the conversation reopens

The PA23 dynamic market is the leading candidate vehicle if the conversation reopens, and the named destination direction under the settled posture (9 June 2026: PSR interim, PA23 destination). NHSE Legal verbal view (26 May 2026): both PSR and PA23 are defensible routes for HomeTest; dynamic markets are PA23-only. Hill Dickinson was instructed on 27 May 2026 to run a dynamic-market workshop and produce an advice note feeding the 22 June SLT playback. PA23 is the procurement regime; the dynamic market is the specific vehicle within it that the programme has been pictured against. It is not yet a committed destination: commitment lands only when the recommendation paper lands at SLT. The options matrix in Play 4 names five candidate routes and the reopen event would re-score them against the conditions at that point.

Dependencies

  • Written NHSE Legal view on the interim legal vehicle. The interim shape rests on the chosen vehicle carrying supplier admission. The legal view is the standing dependency; until landed, the position carries a known legal-interpretation risk that NHSE Legal owns to resolve.
  • Approved supplier list register stood up. Names, scope, admission date, status. Started from zero and populated as suppliers come through.
  • Standard call-off template authored and signed off. Single template that wave entrants use; lighter operational annex tailoring than Beta partner MoUs.
  • Quarterly portfolio review carries the standing reopen check. The reopen triggers above are tested at each quarterly review against live-test count, transacted volume and supplier behaviour, so the interim shape is re-affirmed deliberately rather than persisting by default. Whichever position the FY27 planning round takes supersedes this paper.
