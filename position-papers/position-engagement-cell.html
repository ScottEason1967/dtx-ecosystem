Pre-sales and engagement cell

Engagement runs through an integrated pre-sales cell under sponsor leadership, not through a separate sales team. The cell carries the hearts-and-minds work that precedes commercial machinery. Adopted as a cardinal position by the ECOSYSTEM-BRIEF; sponsor sign-off still to land.

StatusAdopted as cardinal: pending sponsor sign-off
OwnerHomeTest Commercial lead · Programme sponsor
Date taken28 May 2026
Review cadenceOn sponsor decision or quarterly

Position

Engagement with commissioners, partners and senior NHS folk runs through an integrated pre-sales cell under sponsor leadership, not through a separate sales team. The cell pulls together hearts-and-minds, regulatory, clinical and commercial conversations into one coordinated front end. The commercial machinery (Plays 10, 11, 9, 5) engages only after the pre-sales cell has identified, qualified and warmed up a partner.

Posture note

This is an assumed working position rather than a formally endorsed one. Commercial owns it and is presenting it forward as the operating shape. Sponsor sign-off will be sought but the position holds in the meantime. If a sponsor decision later overturns it, the paper changes first and the playbook follows. The assumed-position posture is deliberate to avoid an indefinite "pending" state on something the work needs to get on with.

Rationale

The overwhelming majority of what makes the programme succeed is relationship-building, the road-show, the patient stories and the senior sponsor conversations. A small fraction is the technology and the commercial machinery. A separate sales team would create a hand-off problem at the moment continuity matters most: the transition from "we like the idea" to "we are signing the call-off".

An integrated pre-sales cell keeps engagement connected to clinical, regulatory and commercial reality without becoming a transactional sales function. Commissioners and partners experience one conversation with one team rather than a hand-off to "now we send in commercial". The cell pulls in specialist input (clinical sign-off, IG fit, commercial mechanics) as needed but stays the relationship-holder throughout.

The cell sits under programme-sponsor leadership because the engagement layer needs senior weight to open the rooms it needs to open. ICB chiefs, NHSE Directors and ministerial offices respond to peer-level engagement.

Reopen triggers

  • Sponsor decision overturns the cell architecture.
  • Programme scale grows to the point where a dedicated sales function becomes justified (likely aligns with the framework-reopen triggers).
  • A specific engagement need emerges that the cell cannot cover (industrial-scale digital marketing, for example).
  • Cell leadership has to be re-anchored for organisational reasons.

Dependencies

  • Cell lead role explicitly accepted. The cell needs named ownership; general sponsorship is not operational enough.
  • Programme leadership endorses the resourcing. The cell needs FTE allocation across hearts-and-minds, regulatory, clinical and commercial workstreams.
  • Hearts-and-Minds materials assembled into a pitch-deck kit. See the Engagement materials door. The cell needs the deck to work from.
  • Clear interface with the commercial machinery. When and how does pre-sales hand a qualified partner into Play 10, Play 11 or Play 9. Currently informal; needs naming.

What the cell does, and does not, do

Does: identify and qualify partners, build the relationship, brief and re-brief senior sponsors, run the road-show, coordinate the pitch deck, warm up the commercial conversation, hand the qualified partner into the appropriate engagement play. Does not: sign call-offs, set pricing, negotiate contracts, run assurance gates. Those sit with the commercial machinery, the assurance wrapper and IG/DPO respectively.
