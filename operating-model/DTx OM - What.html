WHAT

What it is composed of, and the world it sits in.

Two lenses on the model. Pick one.

Most operating models try to describe what they are by listing their internal parts. That is true but partial. The model is also defined by what surrounds it: the services it consumes, the bodies that regulate it, the suppliers it contracts, the audiences it serves. Anatomy tells you the inside. Ecosystem tells you the outside. Both matter.

Lens one · inside
Anatomy
What it is composed of. Foundation, overlays, patterns, configurations. The four-level architecture that lets one platform support multiple products (HomeTest, HealthStore, HealthSync) with many configurations inside each, without rebuilding the foundation.
Foundation. The universal layer, split between what the platform builds and what it consumes from shared NHS infrastructure.
Overlays. The eight dimensions where products and configurations diverge.
Patterns. The reusable picks inside each overlay.
Configurations. The live deployments (HIV, PSA, iron deficiency anaemia).

Ecosystem
The world it sits in. The BAU team is small because most operating mass sits outside it. Five categories of relationship describe what the platform builds, consumes, is regulated by, contracts and serves. Holds across all Digital Therapeutics products; entities inside each category vary per product.
Built and run. What the platform builds and staffs directly.
Consumed. Shared NHS services the platform plugs into.
Regulated by. Bodies whose approval shapes the model.
Contracted, Served. The commercial counterparts and the customers.
