Product · HealthStore

HealthStore.

A digital therapeutics service. A clinician identifies a patient who fits the pathway, prescribes the digital intervention, and the patient engages with it through the NHS App or a partnered app. Engagement and clinical outcomes are captured per episode and fed back into the pathway. COPD Alpha is the lead service in design today, with two suppliers onboarded for the Alpha (out of three CTF respondents). The architecture is built to host further therapeutic areas as they come into scope.

1HealthStore in the four-level anatomy
Level 1 · Foundation
Digital Therapeutics platform layer
HealthStore runs on the Digital Therapeutics platform layer: NHS Login for patient identity, NHS Notify for messaging, the NHS App for patient surface, FHIR routing for clinical data, the contracting instrument that admits suppliers (HealthStore uses ACE plus the NHS Standard Contract today; the HomeTest interim shape and any future framework conversation are tracked separately), the IG framework structure, the hazard log structure, the assurance gateway and the SHAPE / ASSURE / CONTRACT / FULFIL / MEASURE capability loop. The platform layer is the same across every Digital Therapeutics product; HealthStore consumes it without owning or building it.
Level 2 · HealthStore overlay
App provisioning, prescribing, in-app engagement, outcome capture
The HealthStore overlay covers the eight dimensions where this product expresses itself distinctively: clinician-prescribed initiation, ICB-primary commissioning, NICE EVA conditionally-recommended software supply, marketplace payments mechanism (designed in Alpha), pathway-level tariff treatment, NICE EVA accreditation route, COPD-specific clinical safety content, NHS Standard Contract with SaaS and data schedules. This is the layer where HealthStore looks and behaves like HealthStore.
Level 3 · Pattern picks
The reusable options HealthStore selects
NICE EVA conditionally-recommended accreditation route. Tier C funding category. Marketplace payment commercial model with marketplace payments mechanism (start, meaningful engagement, completion, outcome capture). NHS Standard Contract with SaaS schedule and data schedule. Supplier model for clinical safety (the supplier and NHS England jointly hold manufacturer responsibility). Each pick is a documented option in the platform catalogue; HealthStore combines them into the configuration that suits the service.
Level 4 · Configurations
Live and planned deployments
COPD Alpha is the lead service in design today. Three suppliers progressed through the lead Alpha procurement. Each future therapeutic area runs as a new configuration inside the HealthStore overlay; the architecture lets a new area come online without rebuilding the foundation or the overlay structure.

3The shape of a HealthStore episode

How an episode runs

A clinician identifies a patient who fits the pathway and prescribes the digital therapeutic via the Electronic Prescription Service (EPS). The patient is offered access through the NHS App or a partnered app. Engagement is tracked through four marketplace milestones: start (the patient activates the app), meaningful engagement (defined per service), completion (the episode finishes), outcome capture (the clinical outcome is recorded). Supplier payment triggers against milestones rather than per activation.

The supplier carries clinical supplier responsibility alongside NHS England. The platform handles identity (NHS Login), notifications (NHS Notify), data routing (FHIR) and the contracting instrument (HealthStore: ACE plus NHS Standard Contract). The commissioner buys the service for their population under the NHS Standard Contract. Clinical safety governance runs through the DCB framework.
