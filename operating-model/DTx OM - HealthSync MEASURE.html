HealthSync · MEASURE

HealthSync at MEASURE.

Measurement of the data flow itself, of clinical signal quality, of patient engagement, of equity, and of pathway outcome

MEASURE for HealthSync covers a different measurement shape from HomeTest and HealthStore. There is no test result event to count; no completed therapeutic episode to attribute outcome to. The measurement is of the data flow itself (volume, quality, reliability), the clinical signal (true alerts vs false), the patient engagement (permission grant rate, revocation patterns, sustained sharing), the equity profile (who can use HealthSync vs who is excluded), and the pathway outcome (whether between-appointment data correlates with better clinical results).

What MEASURE has to capture

Data flow quality and reliability

In flight

Data volume by use case, by consumer platform, by data type. Latency from device to NHS App. Schema mapping quality. Routing failure rates. Without baseline data on the pipeline itself, downstream measurement is unreliable.

Threshold-alerting performance

In design

For use cases with clinical alerting (BP starts here), measure false-positive and false-negative rates of the threshold logic. The threshold needs continuous tuning against real-world outcome data; without measurement, the alerting either over-triggers (cries wolf) or under-triggers (misses signal).

Patient engagement metrics

In flight

Permission grant rate at onboarding (what proportion of NHS App users with a relevant condition enable data sharing). Sustained sharing over time. Revocation patterns (which data types are revoked first, what events trigger revocation). The metrics drive the patient permission UX iteration cycle.

Equity-of-access metrics

In design

Adoption rates by demographic and geographic split, completion rates by deprivation index, consumer-device ownership patterns, digital exclusion proxy metrics. The metrics feed the EHIA discipline at Foundation. HealthSync needs these particularly clearly because of the device-ownership prerequisite.

Clinical signal-to-outcome correlation

In design

Whether between-appointment data correlates with better clinical outcomes. For BP: did the longitudinal signal lead to earlier dose adjustment? Did the patient have fewer cardiovascular events than the comparator? The clinical evidence base is what makes HealthSync defensible as an NHS investment.

Pathway-level outcome integration

In design

Where HealthSync data feeds an active HealthStore therapeutic or a HomeTest configuration, the cross-product outcome measurement is the load-bearing case for “one operating model, three products”. Errol's journey is the canonical example; the measurement of his journey is the demonstration that the model is delivering.

Open decisions and dependencies
Outcome attribution methodology
Attributing clinical outcomes to HealthSync data flow vs other care-team interventions is methodologically hard. Outcome attribution methodology needs sign-off before measurement claims become assurance-grade. Coupling tight with Ongoing Evidence Generation at Foundation.
Equity baseline establishment
Without an equity baseline (who is already excluded from HealthSync because of device ownership patterns), the equity metrics have no comparator. Baseline data gathering at Alpha.
PMS routes for the data flow
Post-market surveillance for the data flow itself: how do incidents get reported, what counts as a HealthSync incident, who is the reporting body. The discipline at Foundation applies; HealthSync-specific shape lands at Alpha.
Long-term data retention and analysis
How long is HealthSync data retained for clinical and outcome research use, under what governance, with what access controls. Sits at IG / Information Governance level.
