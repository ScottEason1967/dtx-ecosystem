HealthStore at SHAPE.

Market development and demand generation for digital therapeutics admitted to the national store

SHAPE for HealthStore is where therapeutic-area selection happens, where the supplier pipeline is brought through NICE EVA, where commissioner-facing demand is built, and where the four commercial principles get translated into a coherent admission pipeline. The model is B2B2C: commercial relationships are B2B between HealthStore and digital therapeutic suppliers, and patient access runs through the NHS App as the front door.

The four commercial principles that constrain what HealthStore can hold

SHAPE activity is governed by four principles agreed at programme level. They are not framing; they are the operating constraint that determines what gets shaped and what does not.

1. Third-party only

Locked

The HealthStore only contains third-party digital therapeutic apps. The NHS does not develop NHS-branded digital therapeutic apps for the store; that would remove the incentive for third-party investment. Where the NHS embeds clinical-pathway software directly (first-party or second-party), it does so outside the HealthStore.

2. Nationally approved

Locked

Every digital therapeutic in the store carries national approval against the four bars: clinical safety, technical quality, economic value, and information governance. National approval drives the price-and-procurement model that ICBs and other commissioners then operate against locally.

3. NHS App integrated

Locked

Every digital therapeutic must integrate and share information with the national NHS App / digital hub. This is what enables follow-up support, targeted communication, effectiveness measurement and PROMs capture. It also draws a clear separation between the NHS digital hub and the digital therapeutic itself.

4. NHS as patient-data custodian

Locked

The NHS is the custodian for patient information. Digital therapeutics digitise standard NHS treatment pathways; they create or use temporary copies of patient information for the duration of the therapeutic episode but the NHS digital hub remains the system of record. Data standards and a code of connection govern what flows between the two.

What HealthStore is shaping

Therapeutic-area pipeline

In flight

COPD is the lead therapeutic area for the Alpha (Use Case 1). Cardiac rehabilitation has been formally endorsed as Use Case 2 (HealthStore Delivery Board, 9 June 2026), running with three engaged suppliers (KiActiv, my mhealth myHeart, I-impact) drawn from a NICE-usable cardiac rehab pool of seven. MSK and lower-back pain is in the pipeline as the candidate Use Case 3 with a July 2026 Programme Board decision. Digital CBT for insomnia (dCBTi) is a further candidate, with NICE multi-technology guidance expected in early 2027 and an internal ambition to include dCBTi in HealthStore by April 2027. Mental health is a future candidate area beyond that.

NICE EVA conditionally-recommended pipeline engagement

In flight

NICE EVA is the route that brings digital therapeutic suppliers to the bar HealthStore admits against. EVA recommendations are conditional: technologies may be considered plausibly clinically and cost effective but further evidence is required. The model and any commissioner-facing material must not imply “NICE approved”; the framing is “NICE EVA conditionally recommended”. HealthStore engagement with NICE shapes which conditions enter the EVA pipeline in priority order.

Commissioner-facing demand generation

In flight

ICBs are the primary commissioner archetype. Demand generation work in design: the commissioner-facing pitch artefact (what the proposition is, how it's priced, why the economics work for the ICB), early-adopter engagement, the linkage between the digital therapeutic and the wider pathway. Charitable sector and Royal-College engagement runs alongside so the right clinical voices carry the proposition into the system.

Evidence-generation positioning

In flight

HealthStore is positioned as an enabler for ongoing evidence generation, not just a marketplace. Strong interest in making admission contingent on suppliers supporting their NICE evidence-generation plans. This mitigates the risk of suppliers benefiting from conditional recommendation status without continuing to generate evidence after admission.

Funding-route alignment

In flight

The Frontline Productivity Programme explicitly includes digital therapeutics as a valid investment option at regional level. Regions hold the prioritisation responsibility for that funding. SHAPE work includes signalling into the funding routes so digital therapeutics surface as an investable option in the right places at the right time.

Mobilisation status — COPD Alpha

ICB Alpha sites engaged

In flight

Three ICB systems are engaged as Alpha sites for COPD. Welcome decks tailored to each ICB cover the proposition, the pricing and admission process, the local pathway integration approach, and the operational mobilisation steps. The three-site Alpha is structured to surface variation in commissioner readiness across different ICB shapes so the framework lands robustly.

Commissioner discovery in flight

In flight

Round-one commissioner discovery is in progress. Three commissioner archetypes are emerging: paying customers (ICBs ready to commission directly), pathway transformers (regional programmes with transformation budget), remote managers (commissioners delegating operational management to partners). The pitch artefact and onboarding pack are being shaped against the three.

Supplier engagement framework drafted

In flight

Supplier engagement framework drafted for COPD Alpha. Establishes how admitted suppliers engage with the platform during Alpha (commercial terms, evidence commitments, data flows, milestone reporting). Designed to be reusable for subsequent therapeutic areas as the pipeline matures.

Respiratory Transformation Programme partnership

In flight

Pathway integration for COPD Alpha runs in partnership with the national Respiratory Transformation Programme. The OPTIMISE pathway is the reference shape against which digital therapeutic admission is configured. Cardiac rehab (Use Case 2) runs with the equivalent national cardiovascular programme / Modern Services Framework, with formal alignment secured 8 June 2026. MSK with its transformation programme; dCBTi with the mental health route.

Clinical engagement round one underway

In flight

First-round clinician interviews progressing. Coverage includes specialist healthcare professionals with digital-therapeutic-centred roles and clinicians in the lead therapeutic area pathways. Discovery learnings from NHS Scotland's national scaling of digital therapies captured and informing the Alpha design.

GATS early discovery signal

In flight

Early discovery report issued to GATS to establish the relationship and de-risk progression to the next assessment gate. Programme-board discovery report cadence aligning with the GATS review window so that progression decisions are made against a coherent evidence base.

Open decisions and dependencies

Entry condition: Medical Device Class IIa or higher

HealthStore expects DTx apps to be classified at Medical Device Class IIa or higher (MHRA) to join the platform with the full service offer. Class I medical devices that appear in NICE HealthTech Guidance are signposted in the HealthStore interface but do not receive the full HealthStore service offer (NHS App integration, funding routing support). Where vendors cannot establish Class IIa or above due to circumstances outside their control (e.g. authorised body capacity), HealthStore may take interim positions on a case-by-case basis. The entry bar is a published HealthStore principle alongside NICE HealthTech guidance and DTAC compliance.

Pathway-level economic case (cross-stage dependency)
The commissioner case for a digital therapeutic lands at pathway level, not at app level. An ICB cannot fund an app out of app economics alone because the saving (avoided escalation, freed clinician time, pathway-step removal) sits across different budget lines from the new app cost. Best-practice tariffs at the pathway level are the unlock. Tariff design itself sits outside this operating model as a separate workstream; the dependency does not. SHAPE work is most exposed to this dependency because it sets the conditions for downstream adoption.
SaMD pipeline depth
The number of SaMD-regulated digital therapeutic apps in the market may be smaller than the model assumes. Strategic risk for HealthStore scaling: the pipeline of admissable products needs to be deep enough to sustain a national store across multiple therapeutic areas. Currently surfaced as an open risk.
Second therapeutic area selection criteria
Cardiac rehabilitation has been formally endorsed as Use Case 2 (Delivery Board 9 June 2026). Three suppliers engaged: KiActiv, my mhealth (myHeart), I-impact — drawn from a NICE-usable pool of seven cardiac rehab platforms. Use Case 3 selection (MSK as candidate) goes to July 2026 Programme Board with NICE EVA pipeline maturity, ICB commissioning appetite and equity profile as inputs.
Demand-generation model with suppliers
Whether commissioner engagement is joint (platform + supplier together) or platform-led with supplier-wrap underneath. Affects the commissioner pitch artefact, the early-adopter programme and the way the four commercial principles get explained at the point of sale.
