Three procurement scenarios. One decision window.

Three routes are live. The route picked depends on the procurement size category (under £50m, over £150m, or SBS framework adoption) and the regime selection (PA23 versus PSR). Each carries its own framework start date. Project Initiation has to begin on 1 July 2026 for the NHSE routes, or 1 September 2026 for SBS. Everything else downstream is paced from that start date.

Operating logic

How the page is used.

Items live on the programme risk register, framed as dependencies. The wider programme-risk review is the operational forum where the items move. This page is the commercial workstream's view of what is on the register. It does not replace the register; it makes the dependencies legible.

Status colour. Burning items need an answer now: the deadline is here or has already passed. Tracked items have a working date set with work in flight to land them. Future items are further out and on the radar. Cardinal items are the four inputs SLT named on 1 June, each of which has to be answered in some form before Project Initiation begins.

Bang the drum. Commercial is the recipient of these inputs; the programme owns surfacing them. Where a date slips, commercial flags it as a programme-level risk with the named consequence. The conversation is not "you have failed to deliver this." It is "without this by the date below, the procurement timetable shifts to a slower scenario."

Updating. The page is live. Items move status as the inputs land. When an input arrives, the operating model gets updated first; this page follows.
