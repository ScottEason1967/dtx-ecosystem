HealthSync Teamwork.

TOM components identified in primary source

The following HealthSync TOM components are identified at structural level:

PHR aggregation layer · the NHS App PHR construct that ingests consumer-platform data.
Apple HealthKit integration · iOS-side consumer platform interface.
Google Health Connect integration · Android-side consumer platform interface.
Consumer-platform interoperability discipline · standards for what flows where, with what permissions, in what form.
BP use case (lead configuration) · blood pressure self-monitoring with consumer-device data flowing to NHS App and care team.
Threshold-based alerting / PIE-CIE escalation · data-driven escalation between appointments.
Patient permission and revocation model · granular controls at consumer-platform layer and NHS App layer.
Federated controller for consumer-platform data · Apple / Google as platform operator, NHS as controller for the NHS App view.
Lawful basis per data type · consent vs public task per use of the data downstream.
Real-world proof-of-concept anchor · empirical demonstration that consumer-device BP data flows into NHS systems and is operationally usable.

Operational shape on this page is deliberately reserved. Surfacing these TOM components as operational lane content / capability sections would require HealthSync-side programme detail and a validator review that have not happened. The Governance Who page carries the source-rebuilt HealthSync RACI; this page reserves the structural slot at the same shape as HomeTest and HealthStore.

In design. HealthSync BAU capability content is not populated. The Digital Therapeutics Operating Model reserves a HealthSync teamwork overlay slot at the same structural shape as HomeTest and HealthStore (numbered capabilities, stand-up vs steady-state breakdown, FTE sizing).

Content lands when the HealthSync programme reaches the equivalent design stage and a validator review has run.
