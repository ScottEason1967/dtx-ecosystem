Layla.

Patient · GP-initiated
“I can't take half a day off for bloods. The kit comes to me, I do it on a Saturday, the GP sees the result.”
42, single parent of two school-age kids, works two part-time jobs (one shift-based). Type 2 diabetes diagnosed three years ago, on metformin. Her GP needs HbA1c every six months. A clinic visit means losing income; a kit at home means she stays in work and her diabetes stays monitored. She is the access story the model has to serve.

Why this role matters

Layla is the third Year 1 patient archetype. Abdul is patient-initiated (he self-screens with the NHS App, CVKM Check Up follows), Mark is clinician-initiated (his consultant requests, PSA-AS), Layla is GP-initiated (her GP arranges, HbA1c monitoring). Together the three cover the SLT-confirmed initiation patterns. They also test whether the model serves access: Layla is the one whose journey to a clinic is the actual barrier. If the model only works for people who can take time off, it isn't doing the equity job.

The third archetype carries the equity load. Abdul and Mark both have routes into care that don't depend on physical access barriers. Layla is the one who can't easily take time off, can't easily get to the clinic, and for whom a kit at home is the difference between staying monitored and falling out of the system. The Purpose statement (better access, earlier detection, fairer outcomes) reads true when Layla's journey works. If she can't reach the model, the model isn't doing what it says on the tin.

What's different about Layla's journey

Layla doesn't order her own test, and her consultant doesn't either. Her GP arranges it from the practice system as part of her diabetes review cycle. The kit arrives at her house, she takes the sample around her shifts, returns it via post, and the result lands directly in her GP record. Her GP only contacts her if something needs action: medication adjustment, lifestyle review, referral. The data flow is GP-system to lab to GP-record, with Layla as the sample provider rather than the orderer or recipient. This is fundamentally different to both Abdul (who initiates and receives) and Mark (whose consultant initiates and reviews): and it stresses a different part of the model: integration with primary care systems, GP-record write-back, GP-led follow-up.
