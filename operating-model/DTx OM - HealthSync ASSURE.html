HealthSync at ASSURE.

Assurance shape for consumer-platform-sourced data flowing into NHS systems

ASSURE for HealthSync covers a different assurance shape from HomeTest and HealthStore: there is no admitted SaMD product at the centre, no kit moving through a supply chain, no commissioner-direct procurement. Assurance is about the data flow itself — whether the consumer-platform data is reliable enough, controlled enough, governed enough, and surfaced safely enough to be useful clinically. The discipline anchors on the federated controller arrangement, the lawful basis per data type, and the clinical safety case for between-appointment signal use.

The assurance shape

Federated controller designation

In flight

NHS holds the controller position for what flows into the NHS App. The consumer platform (Apple, Google) operates the patient-side data aggregation layer. Each party's controller responsibilities are designated explicitly per data type. The federated controller arrangement is the structural alternative to either NHS-as-only-controller (impractical for consumer data) or platform-as-controller (clinically unacceptable for NHS uses).

DPIA architecture per consumer-platform connector

In flight

DPIA scope covers the patient-to-platform flow (out of NHS scope, regulated by the platform's own terms), the platform-to-NHS-App flow (in NHS scope, the federated controller boundary), and the NHS-App-to-care-team flow (NHS-internal). Each segment has its own assurance treatment.

Lawful basis per data type

In design

Different data types carry different lawful basis positions. Some sit under consent (the patient explicitly enables sharing). Others sit under public task (the data feeds direct care under existing NHS clinical relationships). The lawful basis architecture is being designed per data type rather than assumed once for everything.

Clinical safety case for between-appointment signal use

In design

Clinical safety case for the use of consumer-device data in clinical decision-making. Hazards include: false-positive thresholds creating unnecessary clinical contact, false-negative thresholds missing real signal, data quality variance across consumer devices, patient self-management confusion when shown raw signal. The hazard log is being designed against the BP lead use case and extending per use case as the pipeline grows.

DCB framework split for the PHR layer

In design

DCB0129 (manufacturer responsibility) applies to the consumer platform for the data they hold and route; the platform operator is the manufacturer. DCB0160 (deployer responsibility) applies to the NHS as the deploying organisation for the NHS App view and any downstream clinical use. The split mirrors the HomeTest and HealthStore arrangement.

Interoperability standards compliance

In flight

FHIR routing, open standards for data exchange, schema mapping between consumer-platform data structures and NHS clinical record structures. The interoperability discipline is what makes HealthSync extensible to additional consumer platforms or use cases without rebuilding the architecture.

Data quality controls

In design

Data quality varies across consumer devices and measurement contexts. Quality controls cover device class identification (was this BP reading taken with a clinically-validated cuff or a non-clinical fitness device), measurement context (sitting still vs after exercise), and sample integrity (single vs averaged readings). Without quality controls the signal is noisy and care-team acting on it becomes harder.

Open decisions and dependencies
Patient permission UX design
The granularity and clarity of the patient permission UX (consumer-platform layer and NHS App layer) is a load-bearing UX problem. Patients need to understand what they are sharing, with whom, and how to revoke. Design depth at Alpha is point-of-onboarding consent; richer per-data-type controls follow.
Threshold setting and alerting governance
Who sets the clinical thresholds that trigger care-team alerting? The threshold setting needs clinical sign-off at care-team or pathway level. The governance route is being designed.
Cross-platform interoperability scope
Beyond Apple HealthKit and Google Health Connect, whether to extend to other consumer-platform connectors (e.g. specialist health-tech aggregators) is an open question. The interoperability discipline supports extension; the assurance overhead per connector is the constraint.
AI / intelligence layer assurance frame
When the AI / intelligent-navigation layer moves to active build, the clinical safety, regulatory, IG and explainability frame for AI-led decision support is the assurance work that ASSURE picks up.
