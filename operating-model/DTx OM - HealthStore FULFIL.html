FULFIL

HealthStore at FULFIL.

Delivery: from prescription through the NHS App, through use, through outcome capture

FULFIL for HealthStore covers three of the six commercial-journey stages: initial use (digital therapeutic certified and published, gathering further clinical evidence, supporting take-up), full use (fully assured and published, monitoring usage at scale), and update (certification update needed, scoping to either update or re-assess). Offboarding sits at the end of the journey; the model treats it as a structural step that any digital therapeutic in the store can go through.

The fulfilment shape

Prescription by a clinician

In flight

The prescribing clinician selects a digital therapeutic from the commissioner-approved set for the patient's condition. Prescription happens within the clinical workflow rather than as a separate digital-therapeutic-only step. The integration between the clinical system and the HealthStore catalogue is one of the platform-build deliverables.

Patient activation through the NHS App

In flight

The NHS App is the patient's front door. After prescription, the patient is signposted to activate the digital therapeutic through the NHS App. NHS Login handles identity. NHS Notify carries the communications. The patient's pathway is visible to the care team through the same hub.

Service wrapper during use

In flight

The service wrapper is the in-pathway touchpoints that surround the digital therapeutic during the episode of care. Engagement nudges, milestone check-ins, equity-of-access support, signposting back to clinical care where the therapeutic identifies a concern. The service wrapper is a Foundation-level capability shared across the three workstreams in the portfolio; the HealthStore configuration applies it for digital-therapeutic-specific patterns.

In-life outcome capture

In flight

The digital therapeutic captures use data (sessions completed, modules engaged with, in-app self-reported measures) and PROMs at scheduled intervals. The fourth commercial principle means the NHS hub remains the system of record; the therapeutic creates or uses temporary copies during the episode but writes back to the hub via the agreed data flows.

Stage progression within the journey

Initial use phase

In flight

The therapeutic is certified and published. Take-up is supported through commissioner-facing demand-generation activity and clinician-facing onboarding. Further clinical evidence is gathered (NICE evidence-generation commitments at admission feed this). Usage is monitored for the early signal of fit.

Full use phase

Future

The therapeutic is fully assured and published, with full Medical Technology Guidance available. Usage is monitored at scale. This is the steady-state operating posture for an admitted digital therapeutic.

Update phase

Future

Triggered when a certification update is needed — new NHS environment dependencies, supplier-side product changes, NICE EVA renewal cycle, regulatory change. Scoping decision: either update in place or reassess from scratch. The decision feeds back into ASSURE and CONTRACT activity for the affected therapeutic.

Offboard phase

Future

Retirement of a digital therapeutic from the store. Offboarding assurance covers clinical risks (continuity of care for patients mid-pathway) and information governance (data retention, deletion, controllership transition). Offboarding can be supplier-initiated, NICE-EVA-loss-initiated or commissioner-initiated.

Mobilisation status — FULFIL

Service Wrapper anatomy applied to COPD

In flight

The Service Wrapper six-touchpoint chain (Referral / Invite, Login & onboard, In-life Help & Support, Use & Motivation, Signposting / Offboarding, Data return) is being configured for the COPD Alpha. Engagement cadence, milestone definitions and escalation triggers per touchpoint sit against the OPTIMISE pathway. The four local-pathway items (Local use support, Remote / Passive monitoring, PI/CI escalation, Neighbourhood Health team integration) feed the local clinical setting interface.

NHS App integration depth at Alpha

In flight

NHS App integration at Alpha runs at point-of-care prescription depth: clinician prescribes, patient activates through NHS App via NHS Login, NHS Notify carries the communications, episode events flow back to the patient record via FHIR. Richer in-EHR therapeutic-state visibility and prescription-history linkage are Beta-onwards considerations.

Engagement metrics definition

In design

Meaningful engagement definitions for COPD Alpha are being agreed: completion of a defined engagement threshold; symptom reporting cadence; PROMs capture at scheduled points; module-level adherence. The definitions become the trigger conditions for marketplace payment events and feed into the Independent Assurance and Ongoing Evidence Generation pipelines.

Local clinical pathway interface

In flight

The interface between the digital therapeutic and the local clinical pathway sits in Pathway Integration discipline. For COPD this is the OPTIMISE pathway. Each ICB's local pathway team integrates the digital therapeutic into their MDT cadence, escalation routes and review touchpoints. The integration playbook is built once at the platform and repeated per ICB onboarding.

Open decisions and dependencies
Service-wrapper design for digital-therapeutic patterns
The service-wrapper Foundation capability is shared across the portfolio; the HealthStore-specific configuration (engagement cadence, milestone definitions, escalation triggers) needs to be designed against the COPD pathway as the lead therapeutic area, then re-pointed for cardiac rehab and MSK.
Update vs re-assess trigger criteria
When a digital therapeutic needs a certification update, the trigger criteria for “update in place” vs “reassess from scratch” are not yet defined. Affects the supplier's commercial planning and the ICB's contract continuity.
Offboarding mechanics
The procedural and contractual mechanics for offboarding a digital therapeutic. Patient continuity, data retention, supplier exit terms, commissioner notice periods. Designed at the framework level so the same mechanics apply regardless of who initiates the offboarding.
Integration depth with clinical systems for prescribing
The integration between clinical systems and HealthStore for prescribing is sequenced across configurations. Depth at Alpha is point-of-care prescription with NHS App activation; richer integration (in-EHR therapeutic-state visibility, prescription history linkage) is a Beta-onwards consideration.
