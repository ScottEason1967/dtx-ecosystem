Operating risks 11 Delivery dependencies the framework has to mitigate, not decisions to take.
Legal-direction lead time is six months end to end
A new or amended legal direction takes around six months from request to landing. The MI scope and data flows the direction covers must be locked before the request is issued; if scope shifts after issue, the request restarts and the next available answer is two cycles away. Treating the direction as a late-stage compliance step rather than a sequencing constraint blocks the programme for cycles at a time. Mitigation is deliberate MI-scope convergence before any direction request, and a one-shot discipline on what is submitted.
Owner: IG Lead with Commercial Lead and SRO. Standing item on programme board.
Open on HOW →
Supplier off-boarding has no NHSE precedent
The approved list needs a defined process for removing a supplier that fails standards, exits the market or is replaced. There is no precedent elsewhere in NHSE the platform can lift wholesale. Compelled removal is the enforcement mechanism in the interim shape (see Compelled removal position paper). Operational mechanics (notice periods, evidence thresholds, appeals, patient continuity, sample-in-flight handling, residual contractual obligations) all need to be designed. Without it the platform locks in to first-mover suppliers by default.
Owner: Commercial Lead with Operations Lead. Cross-reference to ASSURE off-boarding trigger criteria.

PTT scope is fragmented across clinical areas
PTT teams are organised by clinical area, not by platform. The team that decides directions for the current clinical area is not the team that decides directions for primary care, secondary care diagnostics, or screening outside the current condition. As the platform scales, it engages multiple PTT teams in parallel, each with its own posture, process and timeline. The framework needs to be portable across them, with a clear handover pattern when a new clinical area first comes into scope. Now confirmed: the current screening PTT team adjudicates HIV only. PSA will move to a different PTT team as the platform scales beyond the first clinical area.
