About this stage

Platform-agnostic function. Like SHAPE, MEASURE operates across all products. It is the bookend that closes the loop.

Performance data feeds three outputs: operational improvement (back to suppliers via CONTRACT), strategic learning (back to SHAPE for market and product evolution), and evidence synthesis (for national commissioning decisions and tariff reviews).

Offboarding triggers can originate here when performance thresholds are breached, flowing back to ASSURE for managed supplier exit.

↻
This stage closes the loop. What MEASURE captures becomes the input to SHAPE. Without that flow, the model is linear and static.

Current Status

⚠
MEASURE has no decided items and nothing in progress yet. The whole stage sits as open decisions. Until this is designed, the feedback loop into SHAPE doesn't exist in practice.
Open Decisions 6 items
  • KPI framework not yet designed. Prior DTx experience showed that licence-based KPIs failed because they measured adoption not outcomes. HomeTest KPIs need to measure pathway impact (screening uptake, time-to-diagnosis, clinical outcome change) not just kit volumes or platform transactions.
  • Attribution methodology undefined. Linking a home test kit to a downstream clinical outcome requires longitudinal data linkage that the platform does not yet have. No data sharing agreements or linked dataset access in place for outcomes measurement.
  • Feedback loop to SHAPE not yet designed. The mechanism (what data, in what format, at what frequency, to whom) has not been specified. This is the gap that determines whether the model is genuinely iterative or just diagrammatically circular.
  • Cost-per-test baseline not established. Without a validated cost-per-test across the supply chain (kit manufacture, fulfilment, laboratory analysis, clinical review, IT infrastructure), there is no benchmark for efficiency or commissioning justification. Supplier commercial data from Beta will be the first opportunity. Health economist now scoping component-level costing as initial workstream.
  • Marketplace health metrics (NPS, referral rates, commissioner satisfaction) sit alongside, not instead of, clinical outcome metrics. How the two measurement domains interact and which takes priority in SHAPE decisions is unresolved.
  • Whether NHSE has the internal capability to run MEASURE as an ongoing function. The contracting shape (interim: PSR plus standard call-off plus approved list; or a future framework if the conversation reopens) carries ASSURE, CONTRACT and FULFIL. MEASURE and SHAPE sit with NHSE as permanent capability. Whether that capability is built in-house or outsourced is unresolved.
