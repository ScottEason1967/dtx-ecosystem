Pattern catalogue · deep dive

Pattern overlays in detail.

Plain-English explanation of every Level 3 overlay across the portfolio.

The Level 3 stack on the Anatomical page shows nine overlays across the three products (HomeTest, HealthStore, HealthSync), with picks expressed as short pills (Per-completed, UKAS / ISO, Sex-health svc, and so on). The pills are short by design but the labels do not carry their own meaning. This page is the reference for what each overlay actually decides and what each configuration has picked.

The portfolio sits across three products. HomeTest is operationally mature, with four configurations on or near the model: HIV (live Beta), PSA Active Surveillance (clinician-initiated, prospective), HbA1c (GP-initiated, design phase), and Pharmacy-initiated (working sketch only). HealthStore is in design, with COPD Alpha as the lead configuration. HealthSync is reserved at L4 with the first configuration in design.

Initiation

What this overlay decidesWho triggers the patient activity. The patient themselves, or a clinician acting on their behalf. The initiation pattern shapes everything downstream: who orders, who pays, where the result or data lands, and how onward action is owned.
HomeTestFour configurations across patient-, clinician-, GP- and pharmacy-initiated patterns
HIV
Live
Patient-initiated

The patient orders a test themselves through the NHS App. Triggered by symptoms, a screening invite, a peer recommendation, or self-initiated browse. No clinician request needed.

PSA-AS
Prospective
Clinician-initiated, patient-confirmed (acute)

Hospital clinical staff identify clinical suitability and set the intent to order. The patient confirms the order, address and timing. The kit is dispatched, the patient returns the sample, and the result returns to the requesting clinician. Repeat ordering carries the same confirmation step at each cycle.

Year 1 cohort: radiotherapy follow-up plus a monitoring-gap cohort (men referred to Urology with elevated PSA but no cancer diagnosis).

Persona: Adam, consultant urologist

HbA1c
Design
GP-initiated

The patient's GP arranges routine diabetes monitoring. The result returns to the GP record as part of the standard primary-care diabetes review cycle.

Pharmacy-init
Sketch
Pharmacy-initiated

A community pharmacist initiates testing as part of medication management (HRT, GLP-1, DMARDs / steroids, RA, etc.). Working sketch from the discovery taxonomy. Not on the Year 1 plan.

Underneath the clinician-initiated configurationsHomeTest sits underneath existing clinical recall and tracking systems where they already exist. Where a trust runs a condition-specific tracker for scheduling and recall, HomeTest is the remote-delivery layer beneath it, not a replacement. The tracker remains the source of truth for cadence and clinical management; HomeTest enables the kit, sample and result flow to happen remotely rather than in clinic.
Across clinician-initiated patternsPatient route choice is preserved. The patient retains the choice between in-person and home test at the point of order confirmation. The clinician sets the intent; the patient consents to the delivery route. This applies at every order in a repeat cycle, not only at first order.
HealthStoreClinician-prescribed; lead configuration is COPD Alpha
COPD Alpha
Design
Clinician-prescribed

The clinician prescribes the digital therapeutic to the patient during a consultation. The patient activates the prescribed product through the NHS App or a partnered app, authenticated via NHS Login. Three further HealthStore configuration slots are reserved at L4 (initiation pick TBC per configuration).

HealthSyncFirst configuration in design; three further slots reserved
First configuration
Design
In design

Initiation pattern for the first HealthSync configuration not yet selected. The working frame is patient-managed enrolment through the NHS App with a consumer-platform integration partner (Apple HealthKit or Google Health Connect).

Supply chain

What this overlay decidesWhich supplier delivers which components of the journey (kit dispatch, sample draw, lab analysis, sample return, result delivery for HomeTest; app provisioning, data flows, integration for HealthStore and HealthSync). The supply chain pattern affects single vs multi-supplier framework design, accreditation reach, and where the operational risk sits.
HomeTest
HIV
Live
All-inclusive supplier

One supplier per pathway covers everything end-to-end: kit dispatch, sample analysis, result return. Single point of accountability. Two live Alpha suppliers, each running the whole chain for their LA.

PSA-AS
Prospective
Specialist lab + logistics partner

A specialist lab handles the analysis; logistics may sit with a separate kit-fulfilment partner. Capillary draw equivalence to venous draw is being assessed by the Pathology SIG and the Royal College of Pathologists.

NHS-pathology-first versus parallel mixed-economy supply chain is a live commercial-workstream decision.

HbA1c
Design
Lab-led

A pathology lab leads the chain; logistics integrate around the existing primary-care diabetes pathway. Working assumption pending Use Case 3 design work.

Pharmacy-init
Sketch
Local lab

Local pathology lab serving the pharmacy network. Working sketch. Practical mechanics not designed.

Emerging pattern at L3Collection-first as alternative to delivery-first. Today HomeTest assumes delivery-first (kit posted to patient, patient returns by post). A collection-first pattern (pharmacy or community-hub pickup) removes the 5-7 day delivery wait and addresses unsafe-address barriers. Open question on whether to formalise collection-first as a named L3 pattern alongside All-inclusive.
HealthStore
COPD Alpha
Design
SaaS supplier

The supplier delivers the digital therapeutic as a SaaS product. Patient accesses through the NHS App or a partnered app, authenticated via NHS Login. The supplier holds DCB0129 manufacturer responsibility for the product itself; NHSE holds DCB0160 deployer responsibility. No kit dispatch, no physical sample. Three further HealthStore configurations TBC.

HealthSync
First configuration
Design
In design

Consumer-platform integration partner (Apple HealthKit or Google Health Connect) feeds device data into the PHR aggregation layer of the NHS App. The wearable or biosensor device itself sits outside the supply chain; the patient owns it. The integration partner provides the data pipe.

Commercial

What this overlay decidesHow the supplier gets paid and from which budget. Important: this is the payment to the supplier, not to the commissioner. The supplier delivers the test or therapeutic; the commercial pattern sets the payment shape and source.
HomeTest
HIV
Live
Per-completed-test, LA budget

The supplier is paid per completed test, not per kit dispatched. A kit only counts when the patient returns the sample, the lab analyses it, and the result is delivered. Incomplete journeys do not trigger payment.

Funded from the LA's sexual health budget through the ACE call-off in Alpha; new commissioning route for Beta. Volume-tiered maximum pricing applies.

PSA-AS
Prospective
Activity-based, acute envelope

Activity-based payment wrapped inside the acute provider tariff envelope. The supplier's revenue is tied to the volume of PSA monitoring episodes delivered, paid out of the acute trust budget.

Cost-stack still being modelled by the Health Economist.

HbA1c
TBC
TBC

Genuinely undecided. Could be activity-based, episode-based, or absorbed into a primary-care tariff. Depends on the commissioning route landed (ICB versus GP practice) and primary-care contractual mechanism availability.

Pharmacy-init
Sketch
Per-review

Working assumption: a per-review fee covering the pharmacist consultation plus the test, mirroring the payment shape used for existing community pharmacy services (such as the New Medicine Service).

Underneath all fourCeiling-price model is the drafted commercial approach for the framework or dynamic market: maximum prices set nationally, individual commissioners run their own competitions and price down based on volume. Three-tier structure underneath: mobilisation fee + activity (cost per valid result) + volume floor. Flat per-kit pricing was rejected through the RFI and supplier forums; subscription rejected at strategic level. PSA Active Surveillance Monitoring Episode is a separate model.

First configuration

Live
Commissioner-paid

Free at point of care for the patient. Funded via the LA test-and-treat budget through ACE call-off (Alpha) or new commissioning route (Beta). No national tariff line; sits inside LA commissioning budgets.

PSA-AS
Prospective
Acute envelope

Free at point of care. Diagnostic test sits inside the existing acute provider tariff.

PSA Active Surveillance Monitoring Episode tariff in design. Patient-Not-Present tariff being designed by NHSE Strategic Finance. Glide-path: shadow 26/27, dual-run 27/28 at 50%, live 28/29 at 100%. Five reconciliation mechanisms in play.

HbA1c
Design
QOF / outpatient

Possibly QOF (Quality and Outcomes Framework, the primary-care payment mechanism) or outpatient tariff. Depends on commissioning route.

Primary-care contractual mechanism is not available within the 2027/28 tariff window; HbA1c may fall outside that cycle and require alternative funding articulation.

Pharmacy-init
Sketch

No existing tariff applies. Would need a new payment code under the Community Pharmacy Contractual Framework. Open question pending SLT scoping decision.

HealthStore
COPD Alpha
Design
Tier C conditionally-recommended

Funded via ICB pathway budgets against NICE EVA conditionally-recommended status (Tier C). Patient receives the prescribed digital therapeutic free at point of care. Tariff treatment for digital therapeutics inside HealthStore is part of the broader digital therapeutics funding articulation that runs alongside the per-test PSA Active Surveillance work.

HealthSync
First configuration
Design
In design

Tariff treatment for wearable data integration not yet defined. Working frame: the data pipe is platform infrastructure rather than a per-patient diagnostic, so any funding sits at platform-utility level rather than per-episode tariff.

Accreditation

What this overlay decidesWhich body certifies the lab, service component or product as fit to operate, and to what standard. Accreditation is the regulatory ticket: without it the lab cannot legally analyse NHS clinical samples, or the digital product cannot be admitted to the NHS App route.
HomeTest
HIV
Live
UKAS / ISO 15189

The UK Accreditation Service (UKAS) certifies the lab under ISO 15189, the international standard for medical lab quality. This is the standard NHS lab accreditation. Both live Alpha supplier labs hold UKAS accreditation against ISO 15189.

Mature methodology, no equivalence question to resolve.

PSA-AS
Open question
Capillary draw equivalence

The PSA-AS pathway uses a capillary draw (finger-prick) rather than a traditional venous draw. Whether capillary results are clinically equivalent to venous results for PSA is the open accreditation question.

Pathology SIG and the Royal College of Pathologists are assessing equivalence. Until that lands, the accreditation route for capillary PSA is not settled.

HbA1c
Design
UKAS / ISO 15189

Same standard NHS lab accreditation as HIV. UKAS-certified labs under ISO 15189. Mature methodology, well-established for HbA1c.

Pharmacy-init
Sketch
GPhC + UKAS

Two accreditation regimes layered. The General Pharmaceutical Council (GPhC) accredits the pharmacist as a registered provider of the service. The UKAS / ISO 15189 accreditation still applies to whichever lab analyses the sample.

HealthStore
COPD Alpha
Design
NICE EVA conditionally-recommended

The digital therapeutic is admitted to the National App Formulary through the NICE EVA (Evidence-Generation Assessment) conditionally-recommended route. NICE EVA is the accreditation gate; once conditionally recommended, the product is callable by ICBs through the framework. DCB0129 (manufacturer) and DCB0160 (deployer) safety-case standards apply on top.

HealthSync
First configuration
Design
In design

Accreditation for consumer-device integration partners not yet defined. Working frame: ISO 27001 for the integration partner platform, plus any device-specific MHRA classification on the wearable itself if marketed for clinical use.

Sign-off

What this overlay decidesWhich clinical authority holds the clinical sign-off on the result or output and owns what happens next, especially on a reactive or abnormal signal. Sign-off is where the clinical accountability for the patient lands once the test, intervention or reading has produced a clinical signal.
HomeTest
HIV
Live
LA sexual health service

The LA-commissioned sexual health service holds clinical sign-off. If the result is reactive, the supplier's clinical team makes contact first (per the Reactive Result clinical pathway: five follow-up attempts before escalation). Onward referral routes into the LA sexual health service for confirmatory testing, treatment and ongoing care.

PSA-AS
Prospective
Acute trust governance

Acute trust governance holds sign-off. The requesting consultant urologist sees the result first, interprets it against the patient's PSA history, and decides next steps directly with the patient.

HbA1c
Design
GP / diabetes team

The GP or practice diabetes team signs off on the result. Onward action sits in primary care: adjusting medication, referring to specialist diabetes services, or lifestyle intervention.

Pharmacy-init
Sketch
Pharmacist + GP joint

Joint sign-off. The pharmacist does the initial review at the consultation. The GP holds responsibility for any onward action (medication change, referral). Hand-off pattern between pharmacist and GP still being designed.

HealthStore
COPD Alpha
Design
DCB0160 deployer

NHSE holds DCB0160 deployer sign-off at the platform layer. The prescribing clinical service holds local clinical sign-off for each prescription. Cohort-level safety signal monitoring sits with the Clinical Safety Group against the active hazard log; escalation route to the CSG still being agreed.

HealthSync
First configuration
Design
In design

Clinical sign-off for wearable-data alerts not yet defined. Threshold setting and alerting governance is itself an open decision: who sets clinical thresholds that trigger care-team alerting, at care-team or pathway level. PI/CI escalation between appointments is the named operational pattern, but the sign-off authority is under design.

Result handling

What this overlay decidesHow the result, output or data reaches the patient and what experience they have. Result handling shapes the patient interface: direct via NHS App, mediated through a clinician, integrated into an existing clinical workflow, or surfaced as an in-app milestone.
HomeTest
HIV
Live
Patient-direct via NHS App

The result goes directly to the patient via the NHS App. For non-reactive results, this is the full journey.

For reactive results, the platform-side safety control withholds the user-visible release (status held at RECEIVED, db record set to RESULT_WITHHELD) and triggers the clinical follow-up pathway through the supplier. Reactive results are never given digitally.

PSA-AS
Prospective
Clinician-first

The result goes to the requesting clinician first. The clinician interprets it (especially against the patient's existing PSA history and trajectory) and discusses with the patient. The patient does not see the raw number before the clinician.

HbA1c
Design
GP record

The result flows into the GP record through FHIR-based integration. The GP or practice nurse contacts the patient as part of the routine diabetes review cycle. The patient experience anchors on the existing primary-care review rhythm, not a stand-alone result delivery.

Pharmacy-init
Sketch
Mixed

Mixed model: pharmacist sees the result first and may share with the patient at the pharmacy consultation, or routes to the GP for onward action. Pattern still being designed.

HealthStore
COPD Alpha
Design
Milestone-based

Outputs are episode milestones (activation, meaningful engagement, completion, outcome capture) surfaced to the prescribing clinician through the platform MI feed, and to the patient through the digital therapeutic interface itself. Deterioration alerts and adverse-event signals follow the cohort safety signal monitoring route into the prescribing clinical service.

HealthSync
First configuration
Design
In design

Result-handling pattern not yet defined. Working frame: continuous-data flows into the NHS App PHR aggregation layer with threshold-based alerting that surfaces to the patient's care team between appointments. Patient sees their own data; care team sees alerts when thresholds breach.
