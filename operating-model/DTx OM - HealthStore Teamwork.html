HealthStore operating model in BAU. (HealthStore overlay within the Digital Therapeutics Operating Model)

Sixteen capabilities. For each one, what has to land to stand it up and what it does once running. Click any capability to drill into the detail.

Operating principle. NHSE acts as a coordinating function and collective bargaining entity, not a buyer. The BAU team orchestrates; suppliers, ICBs and clinical teams do the work. Sizing and shape follow from that.

On FTE sizing. The lead HealthStore configuration is in design. There isn't yet enough operational shape to support defensible FTE bands per capability without inventing numbers. Sizing boxes are held at TBC and land once the lead configuration locks. The HomeTest Teamwork page carries FTE bands today because HomeTest is operationally mature; HealthStore inherits the same sizing pattern when ready.

Post-market surveillance is the regulatory obligation that runs for the life of every admitted DTx product. Distinct from the internal Clinical Safety capability (Cap-3) which manages the hazard log and CATR. PMS is outward-facing: signal detection against real-world use, adverse event collection, periodic safety updates to MHRA, and feedback into NICE re-evaluation. The function sits at the platform level so the burden is shared across suppliers rather than left on each one in isolation. Without it, the HealthStore catalogue cannot honestly claim compliance with SaMD obligations beyond go-live.

Pathway integration is where a HealthStore product earns its place in an actual care pathway. Without it, the catalogue could be technically present but clinically dormant. The function sits between national admission (catalogue, supplier onboarding) and local activation (commissioner buying, prescriber adoption). For COPD Alpha it works with the Respiratory Transformation Programme to align the DTx with the OPTIMISE pathway. As further therapeutic areas come on, the same work is repeated with their parent transformation programmes (MSK, cardiac rehab, CBTi etc). The capability also maintains the integration playbook so each new ICB doesn't reinvent the wheel.

Everything the HealthStore BAU team works with but doesn’t run.

The interfaces below are entities the BAU team works with but does not run. Each one already exists, has its own team and runs its own operation. The BAU team manages the interface and consumes the service; it does not replicate any of them. Numbered chips on each card show which of the ten capabilities touch each interface.

Reference
Acronyms and terms used above.

Quick reference for readers who haven’t worked through the operating model in full. Listed alphabetically.

ALB
Arm’s Length Body. A semi-autonomous public body sponsored by a government department. One option for where the platform sits permanently.
API
Application Programming Interface. The technical way one system talks to another.
BAU
Business As Usual. The permanent operating state once discovery and Beta finish.
Call-off
The act of an ICB or other commissioner ordering a configuration under the framework. The contract instrument that turns a framework into a deal.
CSG
Clinical Safety Group. The NHSE body that signs off clinical safety cases and holds the safety standard.
DCB0129
Clinical safety case standard for digital health manufacturers.
DCB0160
Clinical safety standard for organisations deploying digital health.
DPIA
Data Protection Impact Assessment. Required for new processing of patient data.
DSPT
Data Security and Protection Toolkit. NHSE assurance instrument.
DTx
Digital Therapeutic. Software that delivers a clinical intervention or care pathway component.
EScAL
NHSE supplier assurance process. Mandatory gate at admission.
EVA
Early Value Assessment. NICE process for conditional admission of technologies showing promise but lacking full evidence.
FTE
Full Time Equivalent. Headcount expressed as full-time roles.
ICB
Integrated Care Board. Statutory body responsible for commissioning health services across a region.
IG
Information Governance. The set of standards, processes and decisions that govern how patient data is handled.
MI
Management Information. Operational reporting used to run and improve a service.
NHSE
NHS England. The platform owner.
NICE
National Institute for Health and Care Excellence. NHSE body responsible for guidance, evaluation and assurance.
PTT
Privacy and Transparency Team (NHSE). Adjudicates lawful basis and controller designation.
SaaS
Software as a Service. The commercial model under which most digital therapeutics are supplied.
SLA
Service Level Agreement. Performance commitment with a supplier.
SLT
Senior Leadership Team.
SRO
Senior Responsible Officer. The senior accountable owner for the programme.
