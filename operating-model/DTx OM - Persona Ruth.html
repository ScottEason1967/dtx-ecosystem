Ruth is the commissioner who will hold the budget for the PSA Active Surveillance monitoring-gap cohort once that cohort definition lands. Her exact identity depends on the cohort discovery output: acute trust commissioner, ICB-level commissioner, or a different arrangement entirely if the cohort sits across more than one budget. The role is real; the institution sitting behind it is the open question.

What the monitoring-gap cohort is, in one paragraph

The established PSA Active Surveillance cohort covers men with a low-grade prostate cancer diagnosis on active surveillance after radiotherapy follow-up. The monitoring-gap cohort is different: men referred to Urology with an elevated PSA but no cancer diagnosis. Today these men sit between primary-care and secondary-care responsibility. The cohort discovery team are working through how the cohort gets identified, who clinically owns them, what the surveillance protocol looks like, and which budget pays for the monitoring.

Working hypothesis on what Ruth needs

All of these mirror the commissioner-side motivation set, scoped to the monitoring-gap cohort. Specifics will be resolved when the cohort discovery output lands.

  • A clear budget owner. If the cohort sits between primary and secondary care, the budget question has to be answered before procurement can run. The model can't name Ruth's institution until the cohort discovery resolves the funding source.
  • A clinical pathway she can defend. The surveillance protocol for men with elevated PSA but no diagnosis is being developed by the cohort discovery team. Ruth will want clinical sign-off on the protocol before she commits a budget to it.
  • Assurance she can lean on. Same baseline as Sarah and the wider commissioner set: pre-assured suppliers, clinical safety evidence, DCB0129 and DCB0160 covered, accredited labs.
  • A buy mechanism that fits. Once the budget owner is clear, the framework or dynamic market shape will determine whether this is a call-off or a bespoke arrangement.
  • An outcome she can show. The monitoring-gap funding case rests on early detection of the patients in this gap who go on to develop diagnosable prostate cancer. Outcome measurement still to be defined alongside the protocol.

What is held open until the monitoring-gap cohort lands

Commissioning institution. Acute trust, ICB, primary-care commissioning, joint commissioning arrangement, or central NHSE position. All four are live possibilities.

Budget source. Acute provider tariff envelope, ICB pathway budget, primary-care contract mechanism, or new funding earmarked specifically for monitoring-gap surveillance.

Clinical ownership. Whether the cohort sits with Urology, primary care, a shared monitoring service, or a new clinical owner.

Protocol cadence. Whether monitoring is annual, biannual, risk-stratified, or symptom-led.

Contract shape. Until budget owner and clinical ownership are clear, the commercial vehicle and contract structure can't be designed.

Why this role matters now

PSA Active Surveillance is the live clinician-initiated case on HomeTest. If the monitoring-gap cohort lands without a clear commissioner buying for it, that cohort never reaches the platform regardless of how well the rest of the model is built. Putting Ruth into the cast as a TBC role signals where the dependency sits and surfaces the open questions early enough to flow into the Decision Inputs Roadmap.
