Shape, Assure, Contract, Fulfil, Measure

The operating model is not a static thing. It runs continuously through five stages. Shape is where strategy and design happen. Assure is where clinical safety, IG and accreditation are confirmed. Contract is where procurement and supplier agreements are struck. Fulfil is where the service actually runs. Measure is where outcomes feed back into the next round of shaping. Stage 3
Contract
Procurement and supplier agreements. The vehicle through which the centre and local systems engage suppliers.
What this stage does
Selects the procurement vehicle. Admits suppliers to the approved list. Executes call-offs. Operates call-off agreements bilaterally between commissioners and suppliers under the contracting instrument (interim: PSR plus standard NHS call-off plus approved list). Sets commercial terms, payment mechanisms and tariff treatment.

Stage 4
Fulfil
Day-to-day operations. The actual delivery of the service to patients.
What this stage does
Operates the live service. Patient orders kits via NHS App. Suppliers fulfil orders. Lab analyses samples. Results return through NHS App or via clinician depending on the configuration. Logistics and physical supply chain run continuously.

Stage 5
Measure
MI, outcomes, feedback. The data that tells us whether the operating model is working and what to change next.
What this stage does
Captures uptake, return rates, sample quality, turnaround, equity, clinical outcomes. Feeds the data into the next round of Shape. Closes the loop.
