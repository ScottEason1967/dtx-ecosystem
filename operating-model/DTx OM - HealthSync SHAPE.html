HealthSync · SHAPE

HealthSync at SHAPE.

Use-case selection, partner platform engagement, care-team workflow design for the wearable / PHR-anchored data integration

SHAPE for HealthSync is where the use-case pipeline gets selected, where the consumer-platform partner engagement is structured, where care-team workflows are designed around between-appointment data, and where the AI / intelligent-navigation layer ambition gets positioned. The architecture is different from HomeTest and HealthStore: HealthSync runs as a PHR-anchored data-integration capability rather than a clinical service overlay. The NHS App is the patient surface; consumer platforms are the data source; the integration discipline is the load-bearing capability.

What HealthSync is shaping

BP as the lead use case

In flight

Blood pressure self-monitoring is the lead use case. The patient self-measures at home using a connected BP cuff; the data syncs through their consumer-platform layer to the NHS App; the GP sees the trend at annual review or via threshold-based alerting between appointments. The use case is the empirical anchor for the wider model.

Use-case extensibility

In flight

Beyond BP, the architecture is designed to extend to glucose monitoring, sleep, activity, weight and other longitudinal signals. Each new use case adds another data type to the PHR aggregation layer rather than requiring a separate workstream. The discipline is interoperability standards, not product proliferation.

Consumer-platform partner positioning

In flight

Apple HealthKit (iOS) and Google Health Connect (Android) are the two consumer-platform interfaces. The NHS does not procure these in a traditional sense — they are platforms the NHS App reads from on patient permission. The relationship is federated and arms-length: patient owns the data flow, NHS holds the controller position for what flows into the NHS App.

AI / intelligent navigation layer ambition

In design

Beyond data surfacing, the longer-term ambition is intelligent navigation: AI-led signal triage, personalised guidance, contextual nudging. Sits as a Foundation-level capability shared across the portfolio once mature. At SHAPE the architectural slot is reserved; the build sequence is later.

Population-cohort selection

In design

Which long-term-condition cohorts benefit first: hypertension (BP signal), type 2 diabetes (CGM signal), cardiovascular risk, COPD outside of an active HealthStore therapeutic. Cohort selection criteria interlock with care-team capacity to act on the data and with equity-of-access considerations for digital-exclusion-prone populations.

Equity-of-access framing

In design

Wearable adoption skews demographically. SHAPE work needs to identify the equity gap, design support patterns for under-served cohorts, and avoid HealthSync becoming a privilege of those who can afford the consumer devices. EHIA discipline at Foundation applies; HealthSync makes the discipline particularly load-bearing.

Open decisions and dependencies
Use-case prioritisation beyond BP
Second and third use cases after BP need formal selection criteria. NICE evidence base, clinical pathway maturity, consumer-platform data availability, care-team capacity to act, equity profile.
Care-team capacity model
Between-appointment data only delivers value if the care team is funded and structured to act on it. The capacity model for primary-care teams to handle threshold-based alerts and patient-initiated escalations is a structural dependency on workforce planning that sits outside this operating model.
AI / intelligence layer governance
When AI / intelligent navigation moves from architectural slot to active build, the clinical safety, regulatory, IG and explainability frame needs to be in place. Architectural design at SHAPE includes reserving the governance slot.
Validator engagement
Substantive HealthSync programme-content depth lands once a named HealthSync-side validator engages with the model. Architectural content stands; operational status detail follows programme progression.
