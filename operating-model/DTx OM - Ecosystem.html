ECOSYSTEM

Five relationships the platform holds with the world around it.

The platform builds a specific core, consumes shared NHS infrastructure, is regulated by external bodies, holds contracts commercially, and serves commissioners, patients and clinicians. The same five cardinals hold across HomeTest, HealthStore and HealthSync. The entities inside each cardinal vary by product.

How to read this page. Click a cardinal across the top to open it. Each cardinal shows the three product columns side by side so you can compare what HomeTest, HealthStore and HealthSync each have in that cardinal. HealthStore and HealthSync entities marked In design are reserved at structural shape; content lands as configurations and primaries progress.
1
Built and run by the platform
What the platform builds, runs and staffs directly. The BAU team sizes around this layer. Foundation pieces are shared across the portfolio; per-product configurations sit on top.
HomeTest
10 entities
Integration spineIn flight
Orchestration layer that routes ordering, dispatch, results and status across the HomeTest ecosystem.
Framework instrumentIn flight
Commercial vehicle and call-off architecture. In the current window the Step 1 interim route is a six-month SBS PSR bridge for HIV; Alpha/Beta MOUs continue to cover live deployments. Step 2 steady-state vehicle is a provisional dual-route in the draft Recommendation paper (Plan A — PA23 Option B framework or Dynamic Market; Plan B — Digital Primary Care framework leverage; Plan C — commissioner-led direct purchase). PA23 vs PSR not settled in law. See Framework status position paper.

Assurance ModelIn design
The overall HealthStore assurance discipline. Coordinates Independent Assurance, NICE EVA conditional admission, DTAC, DCB0129 (manufacturer) and DCB0160 (deployer) so each is consistently applied across configurations.

Cross-configuration reuse of NICE EVA assurance evidence so an admitted supplier doesn't reassure for each ICB. Coupled to the approved-list machinery and the Assurance Model.

Commissioner-side filters for which configurations apply to which ICBs. Self-assessment toolkit so ICBs can confirm pathway fit, IG fit, prescribing alignment and DCB0160 deployer readiness before engaging the framework.

The marketplace mechanic itself. Defines how the catalogue, payments, milestone reporting and supplier admission interlock as one operational shape. Distinct from the contracting instrument (Step 1 SBS PSR bridge in the current window; Step 2 dual-route Plan A / B / C under SLT alignment).
Contract Management frameworkIn design
Post-execution lifecycle for each call-off agreement. Variations, pricing reviews, term renewals, marketplace milestone evidence audit. Distinct from Framework administration which sets the contracting architecture.
Independent Assurance frameworkIn design
Third-party assurance discipline separate from internal Clinical Safety. Covers regulatory standards, clinical and financial efficacy, independent review of admitted products. Sits inside the Assurance Model.
Ongoing Evidence GenerationIn design
Continuous evidence accrual across admitted products. Outcome data, real-world evidence, NICE re-evaluation inputs. Distinct from Post-market surveillance which is regulatory; this is value-evidence for the Assurance Model.

Why this matters for BAU sizing
The platform BAU team needs to staff Category 1, hold the interface to Categories 2 and 3, manage the contracts in Category 4, and serve Category 5. Being an intelligent client across the four outer categories is itself a substantial job: holding suppliers to SLA, holding consumed services to interfaces, holding regulators to timelines. The team is not everyone needed to run the portfolio; it is the orchestration core. The size question collapses around that.
