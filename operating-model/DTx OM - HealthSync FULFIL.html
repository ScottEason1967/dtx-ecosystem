HealthSync · FULFIL

HealthSync at FULFIL.

Delivery: from patient consent through to care-team visibility and between-appointment alerting

FULFIL for HealthSync is the journey of a single piece of data from the consumer device, through the consumer-platform layer, into the NHS App, into the patient record, and through to the care team. It is also the journey of the patient through onboarding, permission management, and use. The model is different from HomeTest (test ordered, kit delivered, sample returned, result given) and HealthStore (digital therapeutic prescribed, app activated, episode completed). For HealthSync, the delivery is continuous data flow, not discrete episodes.

The fulfilment shape

Patient enables data sharing

In flight

Patient opens the consumer-platform health app on their phone, selects the NHS App as a permitted reader, and chooses which data types to share. The permission decision is granular and revocable. The NHS App holds the permission state on its side; the consumer platform holds it on the platform side. Both layers can revoke independently.

Data flow through Apple HealthKit / Google Health Connect

In flight

On consent, the consumer platform routes the selected data types to the NHS App via the platform APIs. The NHS App is one reader among many that the patient may have authorised. Frequency and granularity vary per data type and per platform.

NHS App ingestion and routing

In flight

Data flows into the NHS App where it is mapped to NHS schema, routed via FHIR into the patient record (Single Patient Record), and surfaced in the patient's view in the NHS App. Routing into care-team view depends on the use case and the configured permissions.

Care-team visibility

In flight

Care team sees the data either passively (at annual review or scheduled clinical contact) or actively (via threshold-based alerting). The visibility model is configured per use case: BP signal goes to GP; CGM signal goes to diabetes team; activity / sleep data may go to no-one until aggregated.

Threshold-based alerting and escalation

In design

For use cases where between-appointment signal matters clinically, threshold-based alerting drives Clinician-Initiated Escalation. For use cases where patient-side signal change drives the patient to seek contact, Patient-Initiated Escalation routes the patient to the right care contact. Alerting governance sits at clinical pathway level, configured per use case.

Annual review with data in hand

In flight

For longitudinal use cases (BP, CGM, weight, activity), the annual review is the moment the longitudinal data has most clinical value. The care team sees the trend, can discuss with the patient, and adjust treatment. The annual review experience changes shape when between-appointment data is available.

Open decisions and dependencies
Patient permission UX maturity
Granularity and clarity of the permission UX evolves through Alpha. Beta-onwards work expects richer per-data-type controls, clearer revocation, and a single coherent permission view across NHS App and consumer-platform layers.
Care-team alerting capacity
Between-appointment alerting only adds value if care-team capacity is funded to act. The capacity model needs alignment with primary-care workforce planning. Dependency on workforce capacity sits outside the operating model.
Use-case-specific threshold setting
Each new use case needs clinical sign-off on the thresholds that drive alerting. The governance route for threshold setting is being designed and will repeat per use case.
Cross-product data correlation
When a patient (like Errol) is using HealthSync alongside HomeTest and HealthStore, the data from all three converges in the patient record. The care-team view of the combined picture is a Foundation-level integration challenge that surfaces in FULFIL.
