About this stage

This is where products primarily live. SHAPE, ASSURE and CONTRACT are largely product-agnostic (with product-specific parameters). FULFIL is where each product looks and feels different.

HomeTest: Kit ordering, sample logistics, lab processing, result return, clinical safety net, patient notifications via NHS Notify.

HealthStore: Digital therapeutic onboarding, licence management, usage monitoring, clinical pathway integration, outcome tracking.

HealthSync (Wearables): Wearable data integration, continuous monitoring protocols, clinical alerting, device management. Not yet scoped beyond high-level concept.

Supplier switching and operational transition mechanisms live here. When a supplier exits, FULFIL manages the patient-facing transition to maintain continuity of care.

In Progress 7 items
  • Supply chain architecture: Notify capability assessment completed 29 April. Notify can do digital communications (SMS, email, NHS App, letters via Royal Mail) but has no infrastructure for physical kit fulfilment, lab linkage or end-to-end logistics. Notify would have to build fulfilment from scratch. Strawman drafted 30 April for Notify to mark up. Likely direction forming: Notify in for comms, parked for fulfilment and labs. All-inclusive supplier model remains working default for kit fulfilment
  • Efficio Consulting engaged as external supply chain advisory (20 Apr). Working group established across commercial, clinical, and programme leads
  • NHS Notify integration for patient notifications: digital comms settled as Notify's domain. Notify works through dedicated Royal Mail Health function (NHS-facing, ~18 months old). Covers large-letter postal tariffs and volume discounts
  • Royal Devon confirmed as preferred acute partner for the PSA Active Surveillance pathway. Engagement window June 2026 to March 2027, mobilising 1 June 2026 under MOU. Working cohort circa 100 to 200 patients on PSA Active Surveillance through the engagement. Royal Devon carries acute self-sample pathways, high-sensitivity PSA analysers and a National Institute for Health Research centre. Treated as external pathology provider, API in / API out. Engaged as clinical partner, not commercial supplier. Reserves identified for backstop and roll-forward but not named here.
  • Beta scope: Private Beta up to 100 targeted tests. Public Beta up to 1,000+, December 2026 to March 2027 window. GDS Alpha Assessment completed 30 April. Private Beta approval being sought
  • HPV Self-Testing architecture as comparator. Prime contractor + 8 regional NHS labs model. 4 million invitations planned year 1, 18% uptake baseline. Procurement failed twice including one legal challenge. Lessons directly applicable to framework design
  • Four pathology supplier phenotypes identified: NHS-supplied pathology (14 networks), NHS commercial partnerships (51/49 JVs), NHS trusts with their own commercial offering, commercial suppliers (~8 HIV-active, 15+ via transdermal RFI)
