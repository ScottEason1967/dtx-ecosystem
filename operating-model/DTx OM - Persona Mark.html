Mark.

Patient · clinician-initiated
“The test comes to me. The consultant sees the result. They only call me if something needs to change.”
65, retired teacher, lives with his wife. Diagnosed with low-grade prostate cancer two years ago and put on active surveillance rather than immediate treatment. Sees his consultant urologist every six months. PSA-AS lets him do the routine monitoring at home and come in only when the consultant flags something.

Why this role matters

Mark is the clinician-initiated patient archetype. Where Abdul represents the patient-initiated journey (he self-screens with the NHS App BP tool and that triggers a CVKM pathway), Mark represents the clinician-initiated journey (his consultant requests the PSA test from the active surveillance list, the result returns to the consultant first, then to him after clinical review). The operating model has to serve both initiation patterns from the same architecture: that is the test of whether it works.

Abdul, Mark and Layla are not alternatives. They are three patient archetypes the operating model has to serve: CVKM screening (patient-init, via NHS App), PSA-AS (clinician-init, acute, longitudinal) and HbA1c monitoring (GP-init, chronic-disease). The model is right when all three fit cleanly into the same foundation, the same overlays, the same pattern catalogue. If we have to fork the architecture to serve them, we have the wrong architecture.

What's different about Mark's journey

Mark doesn't order his own test. His consultant does, from the active surveillance list. The kit (capillary draw, not full venous) arrives at his house. He takes the sample, returns it via Royal Mail to a specialist pathology lab, and the result goes to the consultant first: not to Mark's NHS App. The consultant interprets the value in the context of Mark's PSA trend, decides whether to escalate, and only then is Mark told. This is fundamentally different to Abdul's patient-initiated CVKM screening journey at every stage of the FULFIL lane, even though it uses the same operating model foundation.
