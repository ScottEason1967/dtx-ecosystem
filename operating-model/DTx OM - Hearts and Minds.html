The operating model in human voice

Four stories.
Four audiences.

Operating models live or die on whether four audiences want them to work: the patients who use the service, the commissioners who buy it, the suppliers who build it, and the clinicians who prescribe it. The architecture pages on this site describe what the model is. These four stories describe why it matters, one for each audience, each told through one person you can hold in your head.

Why hearts and minds
Architecture wins arguments. Stories win adoption.
An operating model lives or dies on whether the people inside it want it to work. The architecture pages on this site explain how the model is meant to operate. These four stories explain why it matters to the four audiences whose buy-in it needs: the patient, the commissioner, the supplier, the clinician. Each follows one named person through the operating model in their own voice, with their stakes, and with the failure modes the model is designed to prevent.
For the patient
Errol
Hypertension · managed at home
One patient. Three workstreams. One operating model. Errol's morning, and what the NHS didn't miss. The story of a smartwatch catching what he couldn't, a blood test confirming what the data suggested, a digital therapeutic supporting the response, and a crisis that never happened. Across HomeTest, HealthStore and HealthSync without ever onboarding twice.
