Governance · who is accountable

Decision rights, accountability, escalation

The other three frames describe what the operating model is, when it runs and how it operates. This frame is what makes it a thing people own rather than a thing people describe. Names against each part of the operating model. Decision rights for changes. Escalation routes when configurations or products conflict. Without this layer the operating model is a beautiful piece of architecture nobody is responsible for. Governance described here holds at portfolio level (NHSE accountability shared across HomeTest, HealthStore and HealthSync). Per-product supplier admission and clinical safety governance vary inside this frame.

Portfolio scope. NHSE accountability and the contracting instruments hold across HomeTest, HealthStore and HealthSync (HomeTest: Step 1 interim is a six-month SBS PSR bridge for HIV with Alpha/Beta MOUs continuing; Step 2 steady-state vehicle is provisional dual-route Plan A / B / C with PA23 vs PSR not yet settled in law; HealthStore: ACE plus NHS Standard Contract; HealthSync: instrument pending). Per-product supplier admission and clinical safety governance vary inside this frame. The operational sections below carry the configuration RACI for each product: HomeTest configurations (HIV and PSA), the HealthStore lead configuration (COPD Alpha), and the HealthSync reserved configuration slot.
Roles populated, named accountability still to land
This frame uses roles, not named individuals. It describes who is accountable, responsible, consulted and informed once the operating model is live and running — the end-state RACI. Roles are deliberately not named to individuals on this page; that is the SRO's call once the model is signed off. The architectural RACI rows show Open at SRO level where the end-state owner has not yet been agreed. These are decisions to be taken by the SRO, not gaps in source.
Key open governance questions10 items
Ten things SLT needs to land before this skeleton becomes named accountability. The Open at SRO level cells across the architectural RACI and the configuration-specific tables are pointing at these: making them explicit here.
  • Permanent platform hosting post-Beta. Architectural RACI for the Foundation is open at SRO level. When HomeTest exits discovery, where does the platform live permanently: inside NHSE, an ALB, or a new entity? Owner: SRO with Commercial Lead.
  • Procurement vehicle: three-step decision shape. Three steps land in sequence. Step 1 (interim route to market): six-month SBS PSR bridge for the HIV use case only; Alpha/Beta MOUs continue to cover live deployments. Step 2 (steady-state procurement vehicle): PA23 vs PSR is not settled in law. NHS Legal advice is that a case can be made for either, with the recommended approach being to start from the desired solution and work backwards. Provisional dual-route in the draft Recommendation paper: Plan A — PA23 Option B framework or Dynamic Market; Plan B — Digital Primary Care framework leverage; Plan C — commissioner-led direct purchase. External procurement legal counsel formally instructed to run a Plan A scoping workshop. Step 3 (framework administration route): NHSE direct, SBS, CCS or third-party; coupled to Step 2. The four-option matrix from earlier discovery is now historical context. SLT alignment at the 16 June Leeds playback. Owner: Commercial Lead with NHSE Legal.
  • Aisha's accountability split across initiation patterns. The two configuration RACIs below split clinical accountability differently: Sex Health Service Clinical Lead in HIV, Acute Trust Consultant and Clinical Safety Officer in PSA. Aisha's overarching accountability across both pathways needs an explicit single statement. Owner: Clinical Safety Group.
  • National Assurance Board composition and constitutional layer. Who sits on it? Who owns final authority? How are disputes arbitrated? What is the appeals process? How are standards updated? How are suppliers challenged? Currently a name in the architecture, not a constituted body. Owner: SRO with NHSE, Clinical Safety Group input.
  • National orchestration BAU funding. Who funds the routing, DevOps, interoperability, assurance management, Blue Book maintenance, supplier onboarding, analytics and standards updates at steady state? The percent-fee-per-transaction model covers the platform foundation but the BAU team and its likely cost have not been sized. Owner: Commercial Lead with Health Economist input.
  • Adoption as a named function in the model. Adoption activity is being delivered through the Partnerships workstream. The unresolved question is whether adoption should be surfaced structurally in the operating model itself (an overlay, a foundation capability, a row in the RACI) or stay implicit inside Partnerships. Decision: SLT to confirm.
  • National rollout without local fragmentation. The architecture answers what the model is composed of. It does not yet answer how the model gets rolled out at national scale so that LAs, ICBs and Trusts adopt it consistently rather than each diverging from the agreed pattern. This is execution discipline (sequencing, priorities, governance of variation), not architecture. Owner: SRO with Commercial Lead.
  • Data controller framework. The federated multi-controller model has NHSE as controller for the foundation, commissioner as controller for the testing purpose, and supplier as processor. Joint-controller arrangements have been ruled out. The exact controller designation per configuration is still being scoped, and the lawful basis position (direct consent vs legal directions) is unresolved at SLT level. Affects every DPIA, every DSA and every supplier contract. Decision: SLT with NHS Legal and Caldicott Guardian.
  • IAO appointment. NHSE policy requires the Information Asset Owner to be a permanent NHSE employee. A candidate has been agreed in principle (Delivery Board, 26 May 2026); finalisation is pending the HomeTest transition from Digital Prevention Services Portfolio to At Home and Community from mid-June. Until that lands the IAO position is provisional. Without a confirmed IAO the platform cannot be formally signed off as ready to process live patient data. Owner: SRO.
  • Cost-benefit dislocation at local system level. Home testing replaces venepuncture, but the saving and the new spend land in different parts of the local budget. Pathology networks don't run phlebotomy; phlebotomy sits in clinical services or under the ICB. Without best-practice tariffs at pathway level, ICBs see home testing as a cost pressure rather than a value case, even when the whole-pathway economics are positive. The economic case is the unlock for adoption. Decision: SLT with NHSE strategic finance and Health Economist input.

Where the cast sits structurally
Working assumption: the operating cast (Sarah aside, who is the buyer) sit as part of the BAU team for the platform in steady state. They are not project roles. Joe represents the supplier-side population, Aisha represents the clinical safety function, Priya represents the platform engineering function, David represents the commercial framework function, Ngozi represents the information governance function. Each function exists permanently once the platform is operational.

Senior accountability for those functions sits on a Blue Book assurance board (working name): a constituted body with named individuals accountable for clinical safety, IG, commercial integrity and platform standards at the most senior level. The cast roles are the conduit through which the platform's day-to-day work runs; the assurance board is where senior accountability lives. Both the BAU team sizing and the assurance board composition are open governance questions.

HealthStore-specific governance features

Where HealthStore governance differs from the HomeTest configurations. Same DCB clinical safety framework, same IG framework structure, same NHSE platform accountability. Per-product variation lives in supplier admission and the supplier relationship.
  • Conditional supplier admission. NICE EVA conditionally-recommended admission rather than UKAS lab accreditation. Three suppliers progressed through the lead Alpha procurement for the lead configuration.
  • Supplier governance. The DTx supplier and NHS England jointly carry manufacturer responsibility per configuration. Hazard log and clinical safety case co-maintained. Supplier-manufacturer agreement terms in negotiation.
  • NHS Standard Contract with SaaS and data schedules. Standard contract instrument layered with a SaaS schedule (software-as-a-service terms) and a data schedule (controller designation, processor responsibilities, episode and outcome data handling).
  • Marketplace payment milestone governance. Payments mechanism within the marketplace, designed in Alpha. Evidence audit cadence under the standard agreement. Disputes resolved through framework terms.
  • ICB call-off mechanics. ICB commissioner is the primary buyer. Standardised call-off terms across ICBs. Local CSO holds DCB0160 deployer responsibility per ICB.
  • Therapeutic area selection. COPD as the lead configuration. Selection criteria and governance for additional therapeutic areas confirmed as the lead configuration matures.
Open at HealthStore level. Final form of the call-off contract instrument under NHS Standard Contract with SaaS and data schedules. Tariff split across the marketplace payments mechanism. Outcome capture instrument per service (PROMs or clinical outcome measure). CATR adaptation for software-as-a-medical-device. Supplier joint-agreement terms.

Who decides what when a change is proposed. Used when a configuration needs a pattern that does not yet exist, when a pattern needs to change, when an overlay needs a new pattern added, or when a procurement regime change is on the table. Add a new configuration to a product

Next step
This is the end-state accountability layer for the operating model. It describes who will run, govern and own each part once live. The work to deliver the model into this end-state — designing each piece, agreeing each owner, sequencing the build — is tracked separately as project ownership. Both have to be true: the end-state has to be defined (this page), and the path to it has to be delivered (project plan).
