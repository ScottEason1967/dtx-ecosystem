About this stage

Same asymmetry as ASSURE. Suppliers are held to commercial terms: pricing constraints, service levels, exit provisions. Commissioners get a simplified buying experience: call-off from frameworks, clear pricing, minimal procurement overhead.

Interim contracting shape: The current working shape is PSR plus standard NHS call-off plus an approved supplier list (pending NHSE Legal sign-off). NHSE convenes the system, assures supplier quality and introduces approved suppliers to commissioners; NHSE is not a party to the bilateral call-off. The GPIT three-layer construct (catalogue agreement, framework agreement, call-off agreement) remains a reference if a formal framework is stood up in future, with PA23 dynamic market the leading candidate vehicle.

Procurement routing: HomeTest is operating in the interim commercial shape. The working interim legal vehicle is PSR plus standard NHS call-off plus an approved supplier list, pending written NHSE Legal sign-off. No formal commercial framework is being stood up in this window. The originally targeted April 2027 framework standup has been retired as unachievable in the current planning horizon. The framework conversation reopens at named triggers (see Position paper: Framework status). If reopened, PA23 dynamic market is the leading candidate vehicle.

Options Matrix · historical context: A four-option matrix was scored earlier in the discovery (O1 PSR or PA23 Framework, O2 PA23 Dynamic Market, O3 Notify-style centralised, O4 Digital Primary Care Framework) against 15 commercial criteria. PA23 Dynamic Market led on procedural fit. April 2027 deliverability was a partial against it. The matrix is superseded by the current position: operate in the interim shape now; reopen the framework conversation at named triggers; PA23 dynamic market remains the leading candidate vehicle if reopened.

Scope and lock date. The Options Matrix is a 12-week HomeTest discovery output, not a portfolio-wide procurement decision. HealthStore and HealthSync sit on different procurement constructs and are not in scope here. Sourcing strategy lock: mid-June 2026, draft in flight from end May. Recommendation lands as part of the discovery sign-off.

Broader driver · tariff conversation at SLT level. The Options Matrix sits inside a wider conversation about how Digital Therapeutics pathways are funded at scale, currently live at SLT level alongside NHSE strategic finance. The procurement vehicle question (which PA23 instrument) and the tariff question (best-practice pathway tariff design) are separate workstreams but interlocked: a procurement route that assumes long-run test-economics funding will not survive the tariff conclusion. The lock target above is the procurement question; the tariff question runs on its own SLT cadence and is not gated by this matrix.

Tariff planning window and 2027/28 availability. Planning guidance for the relevant tariff cycle lands October to November 2026, with health-economics modelling having to land in August to September to feed it. The primary-care contractual mechanism is not available within the 2027/28 window — the route is too contentious to use in the time available. The practical opportunity for the 2027/28 cycle sits with PSA Active Surveillance via a best-practice tariff, plus MSK and cardiac rehab via the DTx route. The configurations that depend on a primary-care funding route fall outside this window and require an alternative funding articulation.

Suppliers are contracted tightly. Commissioners buy easily.
Open Q for SLT · pathway-level economics
The commissioner case lands at pathway level, not test level. Cost and benefit sit on different budget lines. Pathology networks do not run phlebotomy; the saving falls with clinical services or the ICB while the new test cost falls with another holder. A commissioner already funding venepuncture cannot fund the switch out of test economics alone — the test reads as a cost pressure on top of an existing line. The unlock is best-practice tariffs at the pathway level, costed end-to-end against the condition pathway. Tariff design itself sits outside this operating model as a separate workstream; the dependency does not. Owner: SLT with NHSE strategic finance, Health Economist input required.
Open Q for SLT · pathology lab dual position
Where do pathology contracts live? Inside the HomeTest framework as suppliers, or outside it as clinical infrastructure HomeTest plugs into? Affects procurement vehicle, tariff treatment, exclusivity terms. Aisha's persona page treats pathology as both a supplier and a clinical infrastructure partner; the contract architecture has not yet picked a side. Owner: SLT.
Open Q for SLT · pharmacy-led prescribing
Where does pharmacy-led prescribing fit in the framework? Different governance regime (Community Pharmacy Contractual Framework), different tariff implications, different IG flows between GP and pharmacy systems. Holding position: sub-area of Aisha's clinical governance pending input. Owner: SLT. Real positioning to be agreed once the GP-init journeys (e.g. Layla's HbA1c) are operationally specified.
