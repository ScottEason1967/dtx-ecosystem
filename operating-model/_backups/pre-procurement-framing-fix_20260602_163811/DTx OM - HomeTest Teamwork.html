The HomeTest operating model in BAU. (HomeTest overlay within the Digital Therapeutics Operating Model)

Eleven capabilities. For each one, what has to land to stand it up and what it does once running. Click any capability to drill into the detail.

Operating principle. NHSE acts as a coordinating function and collective bargaining entity, not a buyer. The BAU team orchestrates; suppliers, labs, carriers and commissioners do the work. Sizing and shape follow from that.

Acronyms and terms used above.

Quick reference for readers who haven’t worked through the operating model in full. Listed alphabetically.

Arm’s Length Body. A semi-autonomous public body sponsored by a government department (e.g. NICE, NHS Resolution). One option for where the platform sits permanently.

Application Programming Interface. The technical way one system talks to another. Used here in the context of FHIR APIs between the platform and suppliers.
BAU
Business As Usual. The permanent operating state once discovery and Beta finish.
CCS
Crown Commercial Service. A framework administration option.
CI/CD
Continuous Integration / Continuous Deployment. The engineering practice of releasing small, frequent changes to live with automated checks.
CSG
Clinical Safety Group. The NHSE body that signs off clinical safety cases and holds the safety standard. Shortened form of the interface entry above.
DCB0129
Clinical safety case standard for digital health manufacturers.
DCB0160
Clinical safety standard for organisations deploying digital health.

Digital Primary Care procurement framework.

Data Protection Impact Assessment. Required for new processing of patient data.

Data Protection Officer.

Expression of Interest. Stage in supplier or partner selection.
FHIR
Healthcare data interoperability standard. Used for clinical data exchange.

Full-Time Equivalent. One headcount working a standard week. Sizing on this page is in FTE bands.
GDS
Government Digital Service. Runs the digital service assessment process.
GPIT
General Practice IT. A pre-existing NHS contracting construct with a three-layer agreement structure (catalogue, framework, call-off) being considered as a model for HomeTest.
