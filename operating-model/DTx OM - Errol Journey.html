The credibility unlock
The smartwatch catches what Errol can't.
Errol takes his BP readings deliberately and sees the numbers, but he's habituated to them. They don't tell him anything new. The smartwatch is the always-on sensor doing the things he's not looking at: resting heart rate over time, nocturnal patterns, rhythm anomalies. The system spots a signal he didn't even know to look for.
Why one operating model
He onboards once. Consents once. One record. One care team view.
Across the journey his identity, his consent, his clinical context, his data routing and his clinical safety governance carry across every workstream boundary. He doesn't re-onboard at any of them. That is what makes this one operating model rather than three products glued together.

Baseline · ordinary life

Errol takes his BP once a week. His smartwatch hums along in the background. Nothing is happening, and that is the point.

After my scare three years ago I started taking my own BP. The numbers look about the same most weeks. I don't really think about it. The watch I just wear.

Errol
What the model is doing underneath
A connected BP device and a consumer-platform smartwatch send Errol's data into the NHS App. NHS Login ties the device to him. The data schedule governs what flows where. The wearable layer at Foundation level means his GP can see the trend at her annual review, or whenever the system pings her between appointments. He never re-onboarded for any of this.

The catch · the watch sees what he can't

Errol feels fine. The smartwatch sees three weeks of climbing resting heart rate and a couple of unusual overnight rhythms. The system spots a pattern he didn't know to look for.

I didn't feel a thing. The week before I'd been a bit tired, but I'm fifty-six, I'm always a bit tired. She called me. Said she'd been watching the watch data and wanted to take a closer look.

Errol
What the model is doing underneath
Rising resting heart rate plus nocturnal rhythm anomalies are recognised early signs of cardiovascular strain in patients with hypertension: downstream effects that show up in passive wearable data before the patient feels them. The HealthSync configuration carries threshold and pattern rules per use case, not just “BP over 160” but “resting heart rate baseline shift of more than X over Y weeks, combined with Z nocturnal rhythm events”. Those rules sit in the operational layer. When triggered, the signal routes to the responsible clinical pathway, which is why Errol's GP responds by pulling up his BP cuff data and ordering bloods. The watch signal is the prompt, the BP and bloods are the confirmation. The catch isn't the BP. It's the secondary signal the patient isn't looking at. The wearable is doing the work Errol couldn't.
👥
What the care team sees
Alert in the dashboard with the pattern shown. Pulls up Errol's BP cuff data alongside and sees a quiet upward drift he'd been habituated to. Decides to call him and investigate.

Diagnostic · the test that confirms what the data suggested

A blood test kit arrives on the doorstep two days later. Kidney function, lipids, HbA1c: the things you check when you're asking whether a hypertensive patient is heading somewhere worse.

It came in the post. Bit of a finger-prick, post it back, two days later the result was sitting in the NHS App right under my BP chart. Same app. Same record. I didn't set anything up.

Errol
What the model is doing underneath
The HomeTest workstream activates without re-onboarding Errol. The same NHS App handles the kit dispatch, the instructions, the return tracking and the result, not a separate app. NHS Notify carries every comms touchpoint under the same identity. The result lands in the same patient record as his BP trend because the IG framework treats him as one patient across workstreams, not as a different patient per product.

What the care team sees
Blood result and BP trend in one view. Picture is clearer. Time for the clinical conversation about what to do.

Prescription · the medication, and the support to stick with it

The GP adjusts the medication and prescribes a digital therapeutic to help with adherence and lifestyle. By the time Errol is home it's sitting in his NHS App waiting to be activated.

She said the medication would do the work but the app would help me stick to it: reminders, breathing exercises, that sort of thing. By the time I'd made another cup of tea it was in the App. Same NHS Login. Tap, in.

Errol
What the model is doing underneath
A third-party digital therapeutic from the HealthStore catalogue enters Errol's care. DCB0129 sits with the admitted supplier; DCB0160 sits with the deploying NHS organisation. A split, not a joint. The Service Wrapper at Foundation level holds Errol between clinical touchpoints with engagement nudges and signposting. The therapeutic was admitted nationally through the three-step selection (DTx approver, commissioner, clinician); his GP is the third step.
👥
What the care team sees
Prescription decision recorded against Errol's record. Three signal streams now in view for one patient: physiological data from the watch, the test result, and now engagement with the therapeutic.

What Errol's story shows

Three workstreams, one operating model.
Three changes, for three audiences.

Three separate operating models would have forced Errol to onboard three times, sign three IG agreements, exist as three patients to the NHS. Three care teams would have to reconcile three views of him. The operating model exists so that none of that happens, and so that the things that do happen are the right things at the right time.

For Errol
A crisis that never happened.
BP under control. No A&E visit. No admission. His annual review is mostly virtual. He onboarded once and consented once for a journey that crossed three workstreams. He doesn't experience three products. He experiences one NHS App that has things in it.
For the care team
Intervention before crisis.
The wearable signal arrived weeks before the clinical event would have. The diagnostic confirmed it. The therapeutic supported the response. Three signal streams in one record, not three systems to reconcile. Clinical time freed for the patients who genuinely need to be in clinic.
For the system
Pathway value, demonstrated.
Value released at the pathway level, not the test line. The case for the next pathway writes itself from the evidence this one generated.
One person. Three signal streams. One record. Intervention before the crisis, not after it.
