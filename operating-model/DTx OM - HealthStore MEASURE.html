MEASURE

HealthStore at MEASURE.

Outcomes, evidence and the feedback loop into the rest of the model

MEASURE for HealthStore answers two related questions. First: is the digital therapeutic working for patients and for the system? Second: what signal does the rest of the model need from MEASURE in order to evolve the next iteration of admission, contracting and fulfilment? The model is honest that this view is the least populated today — much of MEASURE for HealthStore is in design.

What MEASURE has to capture

Patient-actually-used data

In flight

NICE described patient-actually-used data as “gold dust” and one of the hardest data points to obtain. The digital therapeutic captures session-level use through the app. The HealthStore data architecture has to make that signal available at the right altitude for NICE evidence generation, for the commissioner's pathway evaluation and for the supplier's product iteration.

PROMs and self-reported outcomes

In flight

Patient-reported outcome measures captured through the therapeutic at scheduled intervals. Configured per therapeutic area — COPD-relevant measures differ from cardiac rehab measures from MSK measures. Written back to the NHS hub as the system of record under the fourth commercial principle.

Pathway-level outcomes

Open

The digital therapeutic is one component of a clinical pathway. Outcome measurement at pathway level (avoided escalation, hospital admission rates, primary care contact reduction, return-to-work, mortality) is the level at which the commissioner case lands. Attribution of pathway-level outcomes to the digital therapeutic component — rather than to the wider pathway redesign — is methodologically hard. Currently open.

Equity tracking

Open

Who is reached and who is not, by demographic and geographic cut. One of the discovery-stage outcome drivers. The Frontline Productivity Programme links its allocations to system improvements that include equity; HealthStore signals into the same instrument. Tracking method per therapeutic area not yet designed.

NICE annual monitoring signal

In flight

NICE contacts companies annually during evidence generation, asks for a written update, and scores responses (doing well, doing okay, struggling). HealthStore consumes this signal as part of ongoing supplier surveillance. Where a supplier scores poorly, the commissioner-facing visibility of evidence status needs to surface that, similar to how medicines are shown as pending, approved, under review or subject to further evidence.

Where the signal goes

Feedback into ASSURE / CONTRACT

In flight

Evidence-generation performance feeds back into admission decisions for the next iteration of the supplier's product, into renewal of the NICE EVA conditional recommendation and into HealthStore contracting terms. A supplier consistently failing to deliver against evidence-generation commitments is a candidate for offboarding.

Feedback into Commissioner Store

In flight

The Commissioner Store needs to surface supplier-level evidence status so commissioners can make informed choices between admitted therapeutics. Pattern similar to medicines: clear status indicators (pending, approved, under review, further evidence required).

Feedback into SHAPE

Open

What MEASURE tells SHAPE about which therapeutic areas are working, which are not, where the pipeline of admissable products needs deepening, where commissioner demand is concentrating. The feedback loop closes the lifecycle and is what makes the operating model evolutionary rather than static. Currently undesigned for HealthStore.

Mobilisation status — MEASURE

Outcome data capture in design

In flight

Outcome data capture for COPD Alpha is being designed alongside the meaningful engagement definitions. The data flow routes outcome events from supplier to platform MI, into the patient record via FHIR, into the ICB MI dashboard, and into the long-term outcomes database that feeds NICE re-evaluation. The data-return touchpoint of the Service Wrapper closes the loop.

NICE re-evaluation evidence package

In flight

The NICE re-evaluation evidence package per admitted supplier is being designed at Alpha. Real-world data quality standards, outcome attribution methodology, and the cadence of evidence submission are scoped at the platform so each supplier carries the same package shape. Independent Assurance and Ongoing Evidence Generation feed it.

Post-market surveillance routes

In flight

Post-market surveillance for admitted DTx products is regulatory: MHRA Yellow Card scheme for adverse events, periodic safety updates per supplier, lab-side equivalent for any specimen / device elements. Distinct from internal clinical safety oversight. The PMS framework sits at platform level so the regulatory burden is shared across admitted suppliers rather than left on each one in isolation.

Best-practice tariff evidence pipeline

In flight

Outcome and economic data from MEASURE feeds the best-practice tariff evidence pipeline. Best-practice tariffs at pathway level are the structural unlock for ICB funding; building the evidence base inside MEASURE is what makes the pathway-level case credible to HM Treasury / NHSE. Tariff design itself sits outside this operating model; the evidence pipeline that feeds it lives here.

Equity-of-access metrics

In design

EHIA monitoring metrics for the COPD Alpha are being designed. Adoption rates by demographic and geographic split, completion rates by deprivation index, digital-exclusion proxy metrics, clinical outcome distribution by population segment. The metrics feed the equity tracking section of MEASURE and the cross-product EHIA discipline at Foundation level.

Open decisions and dependencies
KPI framework for HealthStore
Not yet designed. Mix of patient-actually-used, PROMs, pathway-level outcomes, equity, supplier evidence-generation performance and commissioner adoption. Different audiences need different KPIs; HealthStore needs a coherent set.
Pathway-level outcome attribution methodology
How to attribute pathway-level outcomes to the digital therapeutic component vs the wider pathway redesign. Methodologically hard; affects every business-case argument the commissioner makes.
Equity tracking method per therapeutic area
How equity-of-access is measured per therapeutic area, given different baseline populations and different pathway profiles.
Form and frequency of NICE evidence-status surfacing in the Commissioner Store
How NICE evidence-generation status (the annual scoring signal) is rendered for commissioners. Frequency, granularity, threshold for status change. Needs careful design so it's informative without being misleading.
MEASURE→SHAPE feedback loop design
The mechanism through which MEASURE signal feeds SHAPE prioritisation. Not yet designed. Without it the operating model is static rather than evolutionary.
