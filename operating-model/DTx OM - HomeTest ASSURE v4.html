About this stage

The asymmetry is deliberate. Suppliers must demonstrate they meet minimum standards before they can operate on the platform. Commissioners face the opposite: barriers are removed, support is provided, the aim is to make participation as easy as possible.

Supplier onboarding: Evidence packs, IG compliance (DPIA, data controller model), clinical safety (DCB0129/DCB0160), MHRA registration where applicable, EScAL assurance framework. Single reusable gateway rather than per-commissioner reassurance.

Commissioner enablement: Ready-made business cases, pathway templates, integration support, dedicated onboarding contacts. Zero friction for a commissioner who wants to offer home testing to their population.

Suppliers face a gateway. Commissioners face an open door.
Open Q for SLT · pathology lab dual position
Pathology labs sit in two places at once. They are clinical infrastructure under Aisha's safety governance (sample analysis, result generation, turnaround SLAs) AND they are suppliers in Joe's contract framework. The model has not yet resolved which territory holds the contract. Open Q: do pathology contracts run through the HomeTest framework as suppliers, or sit outside it as clinical infrastructure that HomeTest plugs into? The answer affects procurement vehicle, tariff treatment, and the assurance bar applied at this stage.
