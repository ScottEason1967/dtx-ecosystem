The anchor for HealthSync

PHR-anchored integration of consumer health data into the NHS App.

The third Digital Therapeutics workstream alongside HomeTest and HealthStore. HealthSync integrates data from consumer health platforms (Apple HealthKit on iOS, Google Health Connect on Android) into the NHS App, anchored on the Personal Health Record. The 10-Year Health Plan ambition is for wearables to be standard in preventative, chronic and post-acute NHS treatment by 2035. The exam question the workstream answers is "what is the benefit for me if I share my wearable information with the NHS?"

Strategic position

Consumer-platform reach. Apple HealthKit and Google Health Connect cover an estimated 99% of UK smartphones. Over a third of UK adults own a wearable device; over two-thirds have used a health app.
Patient-supplied data, not platform-pushed. The platform integrates data flowing from the consumer device to the NHS App; the patient controls what is shared and when. NHSE is the custodian, not the originator.
Free integration support. The consumer-platform partners offered dedicated engineering support at no cost to NHSE for integration work in 2025. The timing window is finite.
Cross-cutting with HomeTest and HealthStore. Biosensor and wearable data flows feed into HomeTest cardiovascular-kidney-metabolic (CVKM) pathways (blood pressure, smart ring, continuous glucose) and into HealthStore Year 4 vision (DTx data integrated to record, including wearables).
Same operating model, different overlay resolution. The four-level inheritance (Foundation / Overlay / Pattern / Configuration) holds for HealthSync, but several overlays resolve differently. There is no kit supplier; the supply chain is the consumer-device ecosystem. Initiation is patient-supplied data flow rather than clinical request.

Lead use cases

Lead use case
Blood pressure monitoring
Patient-controlled BP readings (from a Bluetooth BP monitor or the consumer device camera) flow into the NHS App, then into the patient record where care teams can see them between appointments. Lead concrete use case for the HealthSync build.
Use case
Remote monitoring
Patient-supplied data for chronic conditions, post-discharge follow-up, virtual ward extension. The clinician sees enough between appointments to act when needed and to avoid unnecessary visits.
Use case
Preventive health
Activity, sleep, lifestyle and wellness data shared into the NHS App to inform personalised prompts, signposting and AI Coach interactions over the 2030 horizon.

Proof points

A real-world proof of concept demonstrated the data flow from a consumer-device BP cuff through the patient's phone into NHS systems, providing the empirical anchor for the BP use case design.
The strategic frame for the workstream is patient-led: "what is the benefit for me if I share my wearable information with the NHS?" The architecture decomposes from bio-measurement → biometric sensor → connected biometric sensor → wearable biometric sensor.
The case sets out the case for Apple HealthKit and Google Health Connect integration with the NHS App: longitudinal patient context, clinician visibility between appointments, patient agency in their own data.

How HealthSync fits in the operating model

Foundation level. Inherits the integration spine, the service wrapper, the IG framework, the assurance gateway. The NHSE-as-custodian principle holds.
Overlay level. Initiation: patient-supplied data flow. Supply chain: consumer device ecosystem. Commercial: no per-transaction supplier fee (consumer device is patient-owned). IG: novel data-controller positions for consumer-platform data flowing into NHS records.
Pattern level. Picks per overlay resolve into the BP monitoring configuration as the lead. Subsequent configurations sit at the same structural shape as further sensor types and use cases come online.
Configuration level. Blood pressure monitoring is the lead configuration in design today. Further configurations (sleep, activity, glucose monitoring etc.) follow.
Open at HealthSync level
The data controllership model for consumer-platform data flowing into NHS records. The boundary between patient-controlled and clinician-actionable data. The integration architecture for write-back from NHS systems to the consumer platform. The commercial position on long-term consumer-platform partnership. The relationship between HealthSync data and HomeTest CVKM / HealthStore care pathways at configuration level.
