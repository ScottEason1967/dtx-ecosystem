Marcus.

Supplier · DTx founder
“We’ve spent four years getting the clinical evidence right. Conditional admission means we can scale before that evidence is published, as long as we keep generating it.”
42, founder and CEO of a UK-based DTx start-up of around 35 staff. Background in respiratory clinical research, then a stint at a med-tech consultancy, founded the company in 2021. Has lived the NICE EVA conditional admission process end to end. Carries DCB0129 manufacturer responsibility for the digital therapeutic; NHSE and the deploying organisation carry DCB0160 deployer responsibility. Cares about ICB adoption volume, the marketplace payments mechanism being designed in Alpha, the regulatory bar staying stable, and not getting cut out when the framework matures.

Why Marcus matters in the model

Marcus is the third-party DTx supplier the HealthStore depends on. He builds the digital therapeutic, owns the DCB0129 manufacturer responsibility for it, and carries the NICE EVA conditional recommendation. Without suppliers like Marcus, HealthStore has no marketplace. His commercial model is volume of meaningful adoption rather than per-activation; the marketplace payments mechanism is being designed in Alpha. HealthStore admission is increasingly tied to suppliers continuing to support NICE evidence generation post-recommendation; Marcus has to keep generating evidence to keep his place.

What Marcus tells the model. The HealthStore can only contain third-party DTx apps; NHSE is explicit that it will not develop NHS DTx apps because doing so would remove the incentive for suppliers like Marcus to invest. Clinical safety responsibility runs through the two NHS England mandatory standards: DCB0129 (manufacturer, sits with Marcus) and DCB0160 (deployer, sits with the deploying organisation). HealthStore does not duplicate assurance; it coordinates with NICE EVA, Innovator Passport and DTAC. The marketplace shape means Marcus competes on clinical evidence, integration quality and outcome generation rather than on sales relationships with individual commissioners.

What is distinctive about Marcus’s role

The commercial mechanism is a marketplace payments mechanism being designed in Alpha alongside three to five credible delivery archetypes. The contract instrument is NHS Standard Contract with SaaS and data schedules between the commissioner and the admitted supplier; HealthStore holds the framework. Supplier admission runs through the NICE EVA conditional recommendation route. Clinical safety runs through DCB0129 (manufacturer, sits with Marcus) and DCB0160 (deployer, sits with the deploying organisation). None of these are individually unique to HealthStore, but the combination produces a supplier role that does not exist elsewhere in the portfolio in this shape.
