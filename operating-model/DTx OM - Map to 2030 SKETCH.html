Notes for your reaction

This is the structure. The visual style will tighten in v1 (NHS spec, proper sticky topnav, character portraits as circle images not letters, hover-tooltips on tests). What I want you to react to first is whether the shape is right.

The shape

Time runs left to right. Four FYs plus a faded "beyond" rail. Five lanes stacked: Decisions / Scale / Vehicle / Assurance / Hearts & Minds. The Scale lane is the spine because it forces everything else. The Decisions rail sits on top so the gates are the first thing read. Hearts & Minds sits at the bottom because it threads through everything and your existing character work hangs off it.

Judgment calls I'd like you to react to

Vehicle sequence. I've drawn PSR matchmaker as Years 1-2 and PA23 framework as Years 3-4 with a hard decision gate at the start of 29/30. If Matt's view is that PSR could run longer than two years, the gate moves right. If SLT pushes back and wants PA23 sooner, the gate moves left. The shape doesn't change; the gate position does.
Character placement on Hearts & Minds. Marcus in 27/28 (early supplier confidence), Ravi and Sarah in 28/29 (clinician and commissioner conversion when scaling demands it), Errol's cross-product journey lands in 28/29. This is my draft. If you want them earlier or later, say so and I'll move them.
Wave 4+ for HealthStore and HealthSync. Right now the Scale lane is HomeTest tests only — that's what the spreadsheet covers. HealthStore (Pauline / COPD) and HealthSync (Errol / Mei) are sitting off-map because we don't have wave-level scale data for them. Do we add them in as later waves with TBC volumes, or do we keep the Map HomeTest-tests-focused and treat HealthStore/HealthSync as parallel maps (one per workstream)?
Audience names in the open. I've named Salford, Bradford, Cornwall, Hampshire on the Hearts & Minds lane. Per your spec we don't name individuals in external artefacts, but ICBs are organisations, not people. Confirm you're comfortable with the geography being on the page, or I generalise to "early adopter ICBs", "next-wave ICBs" etc.
The decisions rail granularity. Five gates feels like the right tempo — one per year roughly. If SLT decisions are tracked at a different cadence on your side, we adjust.
Map terminology. I've used "vehicle" rather than "procurement route" because vehicle is the language the OM uses elsewhere and it reads cleaner. "Assurance" is one word for the whole layer (Independent Assurance + NICE EVA + portability + DTAC). Hearts & Minds keeps your existing brand.

What this does to the rest of the model

If The Map lands as the front door, the six content edits I proposed yesterday become the substrate. CONTRACT page reframes to make the PSR-now / PA23-later story consistent. Status carries the honest "what is actually committed today" line. Anatomy callout for the fiscal scale. Why keeps its philosophical role but moves to position two. Hearts & Minds character pages stay where they are but each one gets cross-linked from the Map at the right point in time.

The architecture pages (Anatomy / Lifecycle / Operational / Teamwork / Governance) don't change. The Map is a different cut through the same material, organised around time and sequencing rather than structure and capability.

What I'm not yet sure about

Whether the volume column behind each FY should be drawn as a literal bar chart (visual weight), as currently sketched as a numeric total (clean but less impactful), or as both. I lean toward both — total numbers on top, a thin bar chart behind. Easy to add in v1.

Whether the Hearts & Minds lane should carry the four existing character portraits as actual circular images at the right point, rather than the letter-in-a-circle placeholders here. Real portraits will land in v1. The question is whether they look right at this scale or whether they need a different treatment.

Whether the Vehicle lane needs a third row of micro-text under PSR and PA23 calling out the £-implication explicitly: "no central revenue" under PSR, "c.£250k/year notional levy at 1%" under PA23. Adds weight; possibly adds clutter.

What this is not

This is not a Gantt. It is not a delivery schedule. It is a map of how the commercial argument plays out over time. The dates are real to the spreadsheet; the audience milestones are deliberately fuzzy because earning trust is not a calendarable activity.

React, push back, redirect. Then I'll build v1.
